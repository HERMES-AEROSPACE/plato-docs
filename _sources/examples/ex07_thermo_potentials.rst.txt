=====================================
Example 7: Thermodynamic Potentials
=====================================

Physical Context
================

This example demonstrates the computation of mixture-level thermodynamic
potentials for the 11-species air mixture
(N\ :sub:`2`, O\ :sub:`2`, NO, N, O, N\ :sup:`+`, O\ :sup:`+`,
NO\ :sup:`+`, N\ :sub:`2`\ :sup:`+`, O\ :sub:`2`\ :sup:`+`, e\ :sup:`-`)
at LTE over 300--15000 K at 1 atm. The equilibrium composition is computed
at each temperature, so all quantities reflect the changing species
populations: dissociation of O\ :sub:`2` and N\ :sub:`2` above 2000 K, and
ionization above 8000 K.

Four results are presented:

1. **Mixture enthalpy** decomposed by energy mode (translational, rotational,
   vibrational, electronic, formation)
2. **Mixture entropy** including the entropy-of-mixing term
3. **Species contributions to mixture Gibbs free energy**:
   :math:`y_i(T) \cdot g_i(T)` for each species, showing how the dominant
   contributor shifts from molecular to atomic to ionic species
4. **Thermodynamic identity verification**: the fundamental relation
   :math:`g_i = h_i - T s_i` is satisfied to machine precision
   (:math:`\sim 10^{-15}`) for every species across the entire temperature range


Running the Example
===================

.. code-block:: bash

   # Generate data with plato_equil (partition function path)
   plato_equil -T 300:100:15000 \
       -m 1,13,24,26,27,28,29,30,31 -s 2,5,8,9 \
       --scientific 15 --db-path $DATABASE air11 \
       > air11_thermo_potentials.dat

   # Generate plots
   python doc/sphinx/examples/scripts/plot_ex07.py air11_thermo_potentials.dat

The ``-m`` flags select mixture-level quantities:

.. list-table::
   :widths: 10 30
   :header-rows: 0

   * - 1
     - Temperature [K]
   * - 13
     - Total mixture enthalpy [J/kg]
   * - 24--29
     - Mode-decomposed enthalpy (tr, int, rot, vib, el, formation)
   * - 30
     - Mixture entropy with mixing term [J/(kg K)]
   * - 31
     - Mixture Gibbs free energy [J/kg]

The ``-s`` flags select per-species quantities: mass fractions (2),
enthalpies (5), entropies (8), and Gibbs free energies (9).


Results
=======

.. figure:: figures/ex07_thermo_potentials.png
   :width: 95%
   :align: center

   Air-11 thermodynamic potentials at 1 atm. **(a)** Mixture enthalpy
   decomposed by energy mode: formation enthalpy dominates above 5000 K as
   dissociation and ionization absorb energy; electronic enthalpy rises at
   high temperature as atoms populate excited electronic states.
   **(b)** Mixture entropy increases with temperature; the steps near
   4000--8000 K and above 10000 K reflect the entropy gains from dissociation
   and ionization respectively (more species, more accessible microstates).
   **(c)** Species contributions :math:`y_i g_i` to the mixture Gibbs free
   energy: at low temperature N\ :sub:`2` and O\ :sub:`2` dominate, while
   atomic N and O contribute at high temperature; ionic species (dashed)
   become visible above 10000 K. **(d)** The per-species thermodynamic
   identity :math:`g_i = h_i - T s_i` is verified to :math:`\sim 10^{-15}`
   relative error for all species.


.. figure:: figures/ex07_identity_residual.png
   :width: 70%
   :align: center

   Relative residual of the thermodynamic identity :math:`g_i = h_i - T s_i`
   for representative species in the 11-species air mixture. The residual is
   at machine precision (:math:`10^{-15}` to :math:`10^{-16}`) across the
   full temperature range, confirming internal consistency of enthalpy,
   entropy, and Gibbs free energy for both neutral and ionic species.


Physical Interpretation
=======================

The temperature dependence of thermodynamic potentials reflects the physics
of energy storage and disorder at LTE:

- **Enthalpy** :math:`h(T)` increases monotonically. Below 4000 K the
  translational and rotational modes dominate; above 5000 K formation
  enthalpy (from dissociation) becomes the largest contribution. The
  electronic contribution grows above 8000 K as atoms populate excited
  electronic levels and ionization begins.

- **Entropy** :math:`s(T)` increases with temperature. Two steps are visible:
  the first near 4000--8000 K from O\ :sub:`2` and N\ :sub:`2` dissociation,
  and the second above 10000 K from ionization. Each step reflects the large
  entropy gain when the number of independent particles increases.

- **Gibbs free energy** :math:`g(T) = h - Ts` decreases with temperature
  because the :math:`-Ts` term eventually dominates. The species contributions
  :math:`y_i g_i` show how the mixture transitions from molecular
  (N\ :sub:`2`, O\ :sub:`2`) to atomic (N, O) to ionic (N\ :sup:`+`,
  O\ :sup:`+`, e\ :sup:`-`) character with increasing temperature.

- The **thermodynamic identity** :math:`g_i = h_i - T s_i` is satisfied to
  machine precision for all 11 species, verifying the internal consistency of
  PLATO's thermodynamic data across the molecular, atomic, and ionic regimes.


Comparison of Thermodynamic Paths
=================================

PLATO supports two thermodynamic paths:

- **Partition functions** (default): reference at 0 K, formation energies
  from spectroscopic data.
- **NASA 9-coefficient polynomials** (``-DPLATO_ENABLE_NASA_POLY=ON``):
  reference at 298.15 K, coefficients from NIST/NASA thermochemical tables.

Both paths satisfy :math:`g = h - Ts` to machine precision independently.
Absolute values of :math:`h`, :math:`s`, :math:`g` differ between paths due
to the reference state convention, but relative quantities (equilibrium
constants, specific heats, entropy differences) agree closely.

The plot is generated by ``scripts/plot_ex07.py``.
