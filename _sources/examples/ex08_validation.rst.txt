==============================================================
Example 8: Validation Against the JTHT Reference Paper
==============================================================

This example reproduces the key validation figures from the PLATO reference
paper:

   Munafo, A., and Panesi, M., "PLATO: a Library for the Thermodynamics,
   Transport, and Kinetics of Multicomponent Gases and Plasmas,"
   *Journal of Thermophysics and Heat Transfer*, Vol. 39, No. 4, 2025.
   DOI: `10.2514/1.T7097 <https://doi.org/10.2514/1.T7097>`_

Five validation cases are presented, covering equilibrium composition,
enthalpy decomposition, interaction potentials, thermal conductivity, and
viscosity.


Equilibrium Composition
=======================

The equilibrium mole fractions of two representative mixtures are computed
over 300--15000 K at 1 atm.

**Left panel**: 11-species air (N\ :sub:`2`, O\ :sub:`2`, NO, N, O, N\ :sup:`+`,
O\ :sup:`+`, NO\ :sup:`+`, N\ :sub:`2`\ :sup:`+`, O\ :sub:`2`\ :sup:`+`,
e\ :sup:`-`). Three regimes are visible: the molecular regime below 2000 K
where N\ :sub:`2` and O\ :sub:`2` dominate; the dissociation regime from
2000--8000 K where O\ :sub:`2` dissociates first, followed by N\ :sub:`2`;
and the ionization regime above 8000 K where atomic ions and electrons appear.

**Right panel**: 8-species CO\ :sub:`2` mixture (CO\ :sub:`2`, CO, O, C,
O\ :sub:`2`, C\ :sup:`+`, O\ :sup:`+`, e\ :sup:`-`). CO\ :sub:`2`
decomposes into CO and O above 2000 K, with further dissociation into
atomic species above 4000 K.

.. code-block:: bash

   # Air-11
   plato_equil -T 300:100:15000 -m 1 -s 1 \
       --db-path $DATABASE air11 > air11_composition.dat

   # CO2-8
   plato_equil -T 300:100:15000 -m 1 -s 1 \
       --db-path $DATABASE co2_8 > co2_8_composition.dat

.. figure:: figures/validation_composition.png
   :width: 100%
   :align: center

   Equilibrium mole fractions at 1 atm for the 11-species air mixture (left)
   and the 8-species carbon dioxide mixture (right). Solid lines: neutral
   species; dashed lines: ions and electrons. Compare with Fig. 5 in the
   JTHT paper.


Enthalpy Decomposition
======================

The mixture enthalpy is decomposed into translational, rotational,
vibrational, electronic, and formation contributions. At LTE, the mass
fractions are determined by equilibrium composition, and each mode
contributes:

- **Translational** :math:`h_{\rm tr} = \sum_i y_i \frac{5}{2} R_i T`
- **Rotational** :math:`h_{\rm rot} = \sum_i y_i R_i T` (linear molecules only)
- **Vibrational** :math:`h_{\rm vib} = \sum_i y_i R_i \theta_{v,i} / [\exp(\theta_{v,i}/T) - 1]`
- **Electronic**: from Boltzmann-weighted electronic level populations
- **Formation**: from species formation energies at 0 K

The left panel shows the full-scale enthalpy, dominated by formation energy
at high temperatures (dissociation and ionization are endothermic). The right
panel zooms to 0--5 MJ/kg to resolve the individual mode contributions.

.. code-block:: bash

   plato_equil -T 300:100:15000 -m 1,13 -s 1,2,5,6 \
       --db-path $DATABASE air11 > air11_enthalpy.dat

.. figure:: figures/validation_enthalpy.png
   :width: 100%
   :align: center

   Enthalpy decomposition for the 11-species air mixture at 1 atm. Left: full
   scale showing dominance of formation enthalpy. Right: zoomed view
   (0--5 MJ/kg) resolving translational, rotational, vibrational, and
   electronic contributions. Compare with Fig. 6 in the JTHT paper.


Interaction Potential
=====================

PLATO implements two families of interaction potentials for computing
heavy-particle collision integrals:

1. The classical **Lennard-Jones (12-6)** potential:

   .. math::

      \varphi(r) = 4\varepsilon \left[\left(\frac{\sigma}{r}\right)^{12}
      - \left(\frac{\sigma}{r}\right)^{6}\right]

2. The **phenomenological (ILJ)** potential of Pirani et al. (2007):

   .. math::

      \frac{\varphi(r)}{\varepsilon} = \frac{6}{n(x)-6}\,x^{-n(x)}
      - \frac{n(x)}{n(x)-6}\,x^{-6}, \quad n(x) = \beta + 4x^2

   where :math:`x = r/r_e` and :math:`\beta` is a species-dependent parameter
   (typically 6--9). The ILJ potential has the same well depth and equilibrium
   distance as the LJ potential but differs in the repulsive wall (softer at
   short range) and attractive tail (steeper long-range decay).

Three regions are identified: **I** (repulsive core), **II** (potential well),
and **III** (long-range attraction).

.. figure:: figures/validation_potential.png
   :width: 60%
   :align: center

   Comparison of the Lennard-Jones (12-6) and phenomenological (ILJ)
   interaction potentials. Top: log scale showing the repulsive wall.
   Bottom: linear scale showing the well depth and the three interaction
   regions. Compare with Fig. 4 in the JTHT paper.


Thermal Conductivity
====================

The total thermal conductivity of the 11-species air mixture at 1 atm is
compared with literature data. The thermal conductivity includes four
contributions:

- Heavy-particle translational (:math:`\lambda_h`)
- Electron translational (:math:`\lambda_e`)
- Internal (rotational + vibrational, :math:`\lambda_{\rm int}`)
- Reactive (:math:`\lambda_{\rm reac}`)

The reactive contribution arises from species diffusion driven by
composition gradients and is significant in the dissociation regime
(5000--8000 K). Literature data from Ahtye (1972), Yos (1963), and
Capitelli et al. are shown for comparison.

.. code-block:: bash

   plato_equil -T 300:100:15000 -m 1,17,18 \
       --db-path $DATABASE air11 > air11_transport.dat

.. figure:: figures/validation_conductivity.png
   :width: 70%
   :align: center

   Total thermal conductivity of the 11-species air mixture at 1 atm.
   Solid line: PLATO (standard collision integrals). Dashed line: PLATO
   (electron-shielded collision integrals). Markers: literature data.
   Compare with Fig. 2 in the JTHT paper.


Viscosity
=========

The dynamic viscosity of the 8-species CO\ :sub:`2` mixture at 1 atm is
compared with literature data. Viscosity increases with temperature due to
increased molecular velocity, reaches a maximum around 10000 K, and then
decreases as ionization produces lighter species (electrons, atomic ions)
with smaller collision cross-sections.

.. code-block:: bash

   plato_equil -T 300:100:15000 -m 1,17 \
       --db-path $DATABASE co2_8 > co2_8_viscosity.dat

.. figure:: figures/validation_viscosity.png
   :width: 70%
   :align: center

   Dynamic viscosity of the 8-species carbon dioxide mixture at 1 atm.
   Solid line: PLATO. Markers: literature data from Capitelli et al.,
   Yos (1963), and Amdur & Mason. Compare with Fig. 3 in the JTHT paper.


Reproducing These Plots
========================

All plots are generated by Python scripts in ``doc/sphinx/examples/scripts/``:

.. code-block:: bash

   python doc/sphinx/examples/scripts/plot_validation_composition.py
   python doc/sphinx/examples/scripts/plot_validation_enthalpy.py
   python doc/sphinx/examples/scripts/plot_validation_potential.py
   python doc/sphinx/examples/scripts/plot_validation_conductivity.py
   python doc/sphinx/examples/scripts/plot_validation_viscosity.py

The scripts require ``numpy``, ``matplotlib``, and ``scipy``.
Output data files are expected in ``data_out/`` at the repository root.
