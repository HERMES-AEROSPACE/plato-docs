.. _theory_transport:

=====================
Transport Properties
=====================

This chapter describes the computation of viscosity, thermal conductivity,
diffusion coefficients, and electrical conductivity in PLATO. The theory
follows the **Chapman--Enskog (CE) method** applied to the Boltzmann equation
for multi-component mixtures, as detailed in [Magin_thesis]_ (Chapters 1 and
4) and [Munafo_thesis]_ (Chapter 2). The implementation resides in
``src/transport/`` (18 source files).


.. _transport_ce_overview:

The Chapman--Enskog Method
==========================

The CE method derives macroscopic transport equations (Navier--Stokes) from
the microscopic Boltzmann equation by exploiting a **separation of time
scales**: elastic collisions are frequent (fast), while inelastic and
reactive collisions are rare (slow).

The procedure, following [Magin_thesis]_ (Secs. 1.2--1.4), proceeds as:

1. **Expand** the velocity distribution function around the local Maxwellian:

   .. math::

      f_i = f_i^{(0)} (1 + \epsilon \, \phi_i + \ldots)

   where :math:`\epsilon` plays the role of the Knudsen number.

2. **Zeroth order** (:math:`\epsilon^0`): The distribution is a Maxwellian
   at the local mass-averaged velocity and temperature. For a
   two-temperature plasma [Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 1.39):

   .. math::

      f_i^{(0)} = n_i \left(\frac{m_i}{2\pi k_{\mathrm{B}} T_i}\right)^{3/2}
      \exp\!\left(-\frac{m_i C_i^2}{2 k_{\mathrm{B}} T_i}\right)

   where :math:`T_i = T_h` for heavy particles and :math:`T_i = T_e` for
   electrons, and :math:`\mathbf{C}_i = \mathbf{c}_i - \mathbf{v}` is the
   peculiar velocity.

3. **First order** (:math:`\epsilon^1`): The perturbation :math:`\phi_i` is
   decomposed into contributions driven by gradients of velocity, temperature,
   and composition [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.32):

   .. math::

      \phi_i = -\boldsymbol{\phi}_i^{\eta} : \nabla\mathbf{v}
      - \boldsymbol{\phi}_i^{\lambda} \cdot \nabla \ln T
      - \sum_j \boldsymbol{\phi}_i^{D_j} \cdot \mathbf{d}_j

   Each unknown function satisfies a linear integral equation with the
   **linearized Boltzmann collision operator**.

4. **Solve** the integral equations by expanding :math:`\phi_i` in
   **Laguerre--Sonine (associated Laguerre) polynomials** and projecting
   onto a finite basis. This converts the integral equations into
   **linear algebraic systems** whose matrix elements involve collision
   integrals.

5. **Extract** transport coefficients (viscosity, conductivity, diffusion)
   from the solutions of these linear systems.

The resulting transport coefficients are expressed as solutions of linear
systems of dimension :math:`O(n_{\mathrm{comp}})`, where
:math:`n_{\mathrm{comp}}` is the number of chemical components. All
systems in PLATO are solved by **direct methods** (Cholesky or LU),
inherited from the EGLIB library [Ern1994]_. No external LAPACK is required.


.. _transport_coll_int:

Collision Integrals
===================

Collision integrals :math:`\bar{Q}^{(\ell,s)}_{ij}` encode the microscopic
details of binary interactions. They are Maxwellian-averaged transport
cross-sections [Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 3.1):

.. math::
   :label: eq_coll_int_def

   \bar{Q}_{ij}^{(\ell,s)}(T) =
   \frac{4(\ell+1)}{(s+1)!\,[2\ell+1-(-1)^\ell]}
   \frac{1}{(k_{\mathrm{B}} T)^{s+2}}
   \int_0^\infty e^{-E/(k_{\mathrm{B}} T)} \, E^{s+1} \, Q_{ij}^{(\ell)}(E) \, dE

where :math:`E = \frac{1}{2} \mu_{ij} g^2` is the relative kinetic energy,
:math:`\mu_{ij} = m_i m_j / (m_i + m_j)` is the reduced mass, and
:math:`Q_{ij}^{(\ell)}` is the transport cross-section of order :math:`\ell`:

.. math::

   Q_{ij}^{(\ell)}(E) = 2\pi \int_0^\infty
   \left[1 - \cos^\ell \chi(E, b)\right] \, b \, db

with :math:`\chi(E, b)` the deflection angle for a collision at energy *E*
and impact parameter *b* [Magin_thesis]_ (Eqs. 3.2--3.3).

Physically:

- :math:`\bar{Q}^{(1,1)}` governs **diffusion** (momentum transfer)
- :math:`\bar{Q}^{(2,2)}` governs **viscosity** (shear transfer)
- Higher orders :math:`\bar{Q}^{(1,2)}, \bar{Q}^{(1,3)}` appear in the
  thermal conductivity and thermal diffusion matrix elements


Collision Integral Ratios
--------------------------

Three dimensionless ratios appear frequently in the transport matrix
elements [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs. 2.63, 2.68), [Magin_thesis]_ (Eqs. B.4):

.. math::
   :label: eq_Astar

   A_{ij}^* = \frac{\bar{Q}_{ij}^{(2,2)}}{\bar{Q}_{ij}^{(1,1)}}, \qquad
   B_{ij}^* = \frac{5 \bar{Q}_{ij}^{(1,2)} - 4 \bar{Q}_{ij}^{(1,3)}}
   {\bar{Q}_{ij}^{(1,1)}}, \qquad
   C_{ij}^* = \frac{\bar{Q}_{ij}^{(1,2)}}{\bar{Q}_{ij}^{(1,1)}}

For rigid spheres, :math:`A^* = 1`, :math:`B^* = 1`, :math:`C^* = 1`.
For realistic potentials, these ratios vary with temperature and
characterize the angular dependence of scattering.


Interaction Models
-------------------

PLATO supports several interaction models, selected by the database:

.. list-table::
   :widths: 25 20 35
   :header-rows: 1

   * - Model
     - Interaction Type
     - Source
   * - Gupta--Yos curve fits (default)
     - N--N, N--I, e--N
     - ``database/transport/`` files
   * - Lennard-Jones
     - N--N
     - Hard-coded tables
   * - Polarization / Langevin
     - I--N
     - Analytical + charge exchange
   * - Screened Coulomb (Debye--Hückel)
     - e--e, e--I, I--I
     - Computed at runtime

At initialization, neutral and electron--heavy collision integrals are
precomputed on a uniform temperature grid (200--100\,200 K) and stored
as lookup tables. At runtime, **linear interpolation** is used.

Charged--charged integrals are computed analytically using polynomial fits
to the Mason--Munn--Smith screened-Coulomb data, with the Debye length
evaluated at the local plasma conditions.

Source: ``src/transport/transport_coll_int_neut.F90``,
``src/transport/transport_utils_coll_int_sc.F90``.


.. _transport_diffusion:

Binary Diffusion Coefficients
==============================

The first-order CE binary diffusion coefficient [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.64),
[Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. B.3b):

.. math::
   :label: eq_Dij

   n D_{ij} = \frac{3}{16} \sqrt{\frac{2\pi k_{\mathrm{B}} T (m_i + m_j)}
   {m_i m_j}} \frac{1}{\bar{Q}_{ij}^{(1,1)}}

where *n* is the total number density. For electron--heavy interactions,
:math:`T \to T_e` and the reduced mass simplifies to :math:`\approx m_e`
[Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. B.6b).

Source: ``src/transport/transport_matrix.F90``, lines 47--97.


.. _transport_viscosity:

Dynamic Viscosity
=================

Species Viscosity
-----------------

The first-order CE viscosity of a pure species [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.59),
[Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. B.5):

.. math::
   :label: eq_mu_species

   \eta_i = \frac{5}{16} \frac{\sqrt{\pi m_i k_{\mathrm{B}} T_h}}
   {\bar{Q}_{ii}^{(2,2)}}


Mixture Viscosity
-----------------

The mixture viscosity is obtained from a symmetric linear system of
dimension :math:`n_h` (heavy particles only) [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.56):

.. math::
   :label: eq_mu_system

   \sum_{j \in H} G_{ij}^{\eta} \, \alpha_j^{\eta} = X_i, \qquad
   \eta = \sum_{i \in H} \alpha_i^{\eta} \, X_i

The matrix elements (first-order Sonine) are [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs.
2.57--2.58), [Magin_thesis]_ (Eqs. C.6a--b):

**Off-diagonal** (:math:`i \neq j`):

.. math::
   :label: eq_G_mu_offdiag

   G_{ij}^{\eta} = \frac{X_i X_j}{n D_{ij} (m_i + m_j)}
   \left(\tfrac{6}{5} A_{ij}^* - 2\right)

**Diagonal:**

.. math::
   :label: eq_G_mu_diag

   G_{ii}^{\eta} = \frac{X_i^2}{\eta_i}
   + \sum_{j \neq i} \frac{X_i X_j}{n D_{ij} (m_i + m_j)}
   \left(\tfrac{6}{5} \frac{m_j}{m_i} A_{ij}^* + 2\right)

The system is solved by **Cholesky decomposition** (``solve_sym_linsys``
from ``src/numerics/``).

.. note::

   In the source code (``src/transport/transport_matrix.F90``, lines
   134--166), the matrix elements are assembled using the factor
   :math:`0.6 A^* = \tfrac{3}{5} A^*`. The off-diagonal element is
   computed as ``fac*(-1 + 0.6*A*)`` which equals
   :math:`(\tfrac{6}{5} A^* - 2)/2` times the common factor --- this is
   equivalent to :eq:`eq_G_mu_offdiag` after accounting for the different
   grouping of constants.


.. _transport_conductivity:

Thermal Conductivity
====================

PLATO computes three contributions to the total thermal conductivity:

.. math::
   :label: eq_lambda_total

   \lambda = \lambda_h^{\mathrm{tr}} + \lambda^{\mathrm{int}} + \lambda_e


Heavy Translational Conductivity
---------------------------------

Computed from a symmetric :math:`n_h \times n_h` system analogous to
viscosity [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.60):

.. math::
   :label: eq_lambda_system

   \sum_{j \in H} G_{ij}^{\lambda} \, \alpha_j^{\lambda} = X_i, \qquad
   \lambda_h^{\mathrm{tr}} = \sum_{i \in H} \alpha_i^{\lambda} \, X_i

The matrix elements (second-order Sonine) are [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs.
2.61--2.62), [Magin_thesis]_ (Eqs. C.6c--d):

**Off-diagonal** (:math:`i \neq j`), defining
:math:`\hat{m}_{ij} = m_i/(m_i + m_j)`:

.. math::
   :label: eq_G_lam_offdiag

   G_{ij}^{\lambda} = \frac{1}{25 \, k_{\mathrm{B}}}
   \frac{X_i X_j}{n D_{ij}}
   \hat{m}_{ij} \hat{m}_{ji}
   \left(16 A_{ij}^* + 12 B_{ij}^* - 55\right)

**Diagonal:**

.. math::
   :label: eq_G_lam_diag

   G_{ii}^{\lambda} = \frac{X_i^2}{\lambda_i^{\mathrm{tr}}}
   + \sum_{j \neq i} \frac{1}{25 \, k_{\mathrm{B}}}
   \frac{X_i X_j}{n D_{ij}}
   \left[30 \hat{m}_{ij}^2 + 16 A_{ij}^* \hat{m}_{ij} \hat{m}_{ji}
   + (25 - 12 B_{ij}^*) \hat{m}_{ji}^2\right]

where :math:`\lambda_i^{\mathrm{tr}} = \tfrac{15}{4} \eta_i R_i` is the
species translational conductivity.

Source: ``src/transport/transport_matrix.F90``, lines 267--273.


Internal Conductivity (Eucken Correction)
------------------------------------------

The internal (rotational + vibrational + electronic) contribution uses the
generalized Eucken approximation [Magin_thesis]_ (Eqs. 2.13a--c),
[Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 3.114):

.. math::
   :label: eq_lambda_int

   \lambda^{\mathrm{int}} = \frac{n_d}{N_A}
   \sum_{i \in H_p} \frac{c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{int}} \, m_i \, X_i}
   {\displaystyle\sum_{j \in H} X_j / D_{ij}}

where :math:`c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{int}} = c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{rot}} + c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{vib}} + c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{el}}`
and the sum is over polyatomic species :math:`H_p`.

The physical picture: internal energy is transported by **molecular
diffusion** rather than by thermal conduction. The Eucken correction converts
the diffusion coefficient into an effective thermal conductivity for internal
modes.

Source: ``src/transport/transport_prop_lam_int.F90``.


Electron Thermal Conductivity
-------------------------------

Third-order Sonine polynomial approximation [Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 4.4b):

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_lambda_e

   \lambda_e = \frac{75}{8} k_{\mathrm{B}}
   \sqrt{\frac{2\pi R_u}{m_e}} \;
   \frac{X_e \sqrt{T_e}}
   {\Lambda_{ee}^{11} - (\Lambda_{ee}^{12})^2 / \Lambda_{ee}^{22}}

where :math:`\Lambda_{ee}^{pq}` are the electron--electron transport system
matrix elements defined in terms of collision integral combinations
[Magin_thesis]_ (Eqs. C.5c, C.5d, C.5f).

Source: ``src/transport/transport_prop_lam_sig_e.F90``, lines 81--84.


.. _transport_stefan_maxwell:

Stefan--Maxwell Diffusion Equations
=====================================

The species diffusion velocities are obtained from the Stefan--Maxwell
equations, which express the balance between driving forces and
inter-species friction.


Neutral Mixture
---------------

For a neutral mixture [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs. 2.69--2.72):

.. math::
   :label: eq_stefan_maxwell

   \sum_{j \in S} G_{ij}^{D} \, V_j^d = -\hat{d}_i, \qquad i \in S

with the mass conservation constraint :math:`\sum_j \rho_j V_j^d = 0`.

The driving force :math:`\hat{d}_i` includes mole fraction gradients and
pressure diffusion:

.. math::

   \hat{d}_i = \nabla X_i + (X_i - y_i) \nabla \ln p

The matrix elements are [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs. 2.71--2.72):

.. math::

   G_{ij}^{D} = -\frac{X_i X_j}{D_{ij}} \;\; (i \neq j), \qquad
   G_{ii}^{D} = \sum_{k \neq i} \frac{X_i X_k}{D_{ik}}

The matrix is singular (all row sums vanish), so it is regularized with a
mass-conservation term [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.73):
:math:`\tilde{G}_{ij}^D = G_{ij}^D + \tilde{a} \rho_i \rho_j`.
The regularized symmetric system is solved by Cholesky decomposition.


Ionized Mixture (Ambipolar Diffusion)
--------------------------------------

For ionized mixtures, the Stefan--Maxwell system is extended with an
**ambipolar electric field** that enforces charge neutrality
[Magin_thesis]_ (Eqs. 1.94--1.95). The system becomes
:math:`(n_{\mathrm{comp}} + 1) \times (n_{\mathrm{comp}} + 1)`:

.. math::
   :label: eq_SM_ion

   \sum_{j \in S} G_{ij}^{D} V_j^d + \kappa_i \, E_a = -d_i'

with the **zero-current constraint** as the additional equation.

The electric field coupling coefficients are:

.. math::

   \kappa_i = \frac{1}{k_{\mathrm{B}} T_h}
   \left(X_i q_i - y_i \sum_j X_j q_j\right)

where :math:`q_i` is the charge of species *i*.

For electron rows, the driving force is scaled by :math:`T_h/T_e` to account
for the different electron temperature [Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 1.90b).

This **non-symmetric** system is solved by **LU decomposition** with
partial pivoting (Crout's algorithm from ``src/numerics/``).

Source: ``src/transport/transport_flux.F90``,
``src/transport/transport_matrix.F90``, lines 343--436.


Thermal Diffusion (Soret Effect)
---------------------------------

In addition to composition and pressure-driven diffusion, PLATO includes
**thermal diffusion** (Soret effect): species diffuse along temperature
gradients. The thermal diffusion ratios :math:`k_{T,i}^h` appear in the
full diffusion driving force [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.65):

.. math::

   k_{T,i}^h = \frac{5}{2} \sum_{j \in H} \Lambda_{ij}^{01}
   \alpha_j^{\lambda}

where :math:`\Lambda_{ij}^{01}` are the off-diagonal thermal diffusion
matrix elements [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs. 2.66--2.67), [Magin_thesis]_
(Eqs. C.6e--f):

.. math::

   \Lambda_{ij}^{01} = \frac{1}{25 k_{\mathrm{B}}}
   \frac{X_i X_j}{n D_{ij}} \frac{m_i}{m_i + m_j}
   (12 C_{ij}^* - 10) \qquad (i \neq j)


.. _transport_electrical:

Electrical Conductivity
=======================

Second-order Sonine polynomial approximation (default)
[Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 4.11b):

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_sigma_e

   \sigma_e = \frac{4}{25} \frac{(X_e \, e)^2}{k_{\mathrm{B}}^2 \, T_e}
   \frac{1}{\Lambda_{ee}^{00} - (\Lambda_{ee}^{01})^2 / \Lambda_{ee}^{11}}

where the denominator is the Schur complement of the 2\ :math:`\times`\ 2
Sonine system.

A third-order approximation is also available, where a :math:`3 \times 3`
symmetric system of :math:`\Lambda_{ee}^{pq}` is solved [Magin_thesis]_
(Sec. 4.3).

Source: ``src/transport/transport_prop_lam_sig_e.F90``, lines 91--108.
