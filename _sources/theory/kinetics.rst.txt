.. _theory_kinetics:

=================
Chemical Kinetics
=================

This chapter describes how PLATO computes chemical source terms (mass
production rates and energy exchange), the rate coefficient models, and the
analytical Jacobian. The presentation follows [Munafo_thesis]_ (Chapter 2)
for the state-to-state formulation, [Munafo_thesis]_ (Chapter 3) for the
multi-temperature and coarse-grained models, and [MunafoPanesi2025]_ for the
complete implementation. The code resides in ``src/kinetics/`` (22 files).


.. _kinetics_rate:

Rate Coefficients
=================

Forward Rate: Modified Arrhenius Law
-------------------------------------

All forward rate coefficients in PLATO use the modified Arrhenius form:

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_arrhenius

   k_f(T) = A \, T^n \exp\!\left(-\frac{E_a}{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}\right)
   = A \exp\!\left(n \ln T - \frac{\theta_a}{T}\right)

where:

- :math:`A` is the pre-exponential factor
- :math:`n` is the temperature exponent
- :math:`\theta_a = E_a / k_{\mathrm{B}}` is the activation temperature (K)

The three parameters :math:`(A, n, \theta_a)` are read from the kinetics
database files in ``database/kinetics/``.


Activation Temperature for Multi-Temperature Models
-----------------------------------------------------

For dissociation reactions in the multi-temperature (Park) model, the
activation temperature uses a **geometric average**:

.. math::
   :label: eq_park_T

   T_{T_v} = T_h^{1-\alpha} \, T_v^{\alpha}

where :math:`\alpha` is the Park parameter (typically 0.3--0.5). This appears
in reaction types ``Dh_cat_mt`` (ID 2).

The temperature derivative is:

.. math::

   \frac{\partial T_{T_v}}{\partial T_h} = (1-\alpha) \frac{T_{T_v}}{T_h},
   \qquad
   \frac{\partial T_{T_v}}{\partial T_v} = \alpha \frac{T_{T_v}}{T_v}

Source: ``src/kinetics/kinetics_rate.F90``.


Backward Rate: Microreversibility
-----------------------------------

Backward rate coefficients are **never** read from the database. They are
always computed from the **principle of detailed balance**
(microreversibility): at equilibrium, every microscopic process is exactly
balanced by its reverse. This fundamental constraint relates forward and
backward rates [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs. 2.80--2.81).

The macroscopic backward rate is:

.. math::
   :label: eq_kb

   k_b(T) = \frac{k_f(T)}{K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)}

**Derivation from microreversibility.** Consider a state-specific
dissociation reaction :math:`\mathrm{AB}(i) + \mathrm{M} \to \mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B} + \mathrm{M}`.
At the microscopic level, detailed balance requires [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq.
2.80):

.. math::

   \frac{k_i^{\mathrm{rec}}}{k_i^{\mathrm{dis}}}
   = \frac{g_i \, Q_{\mathrm{AB}}^{\mathrm{tra}}}
   {g_{\mathrm{A}} \, Q_{\mathrm{A}}^{\mathrm{tra}}
   \cdot g_{\mathrm{B}} \, Q_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{tra}}}
   \exp\!\left(\frac{E_{\mathrm{A}} + E_{\mathrm{B}} - E_i}{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}\right)

Summing over all internal levels *i* with Boltzmann weights yields the
macroscopic equilibrium constant [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 3.48):

.. math::
   :label: eq_Keq_Q

   K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T) =
   \frac{Q_{\mathrm{AB}}^{\mathrm{tra}} \, Q_{\mathrm{AB}}^{\mathrm{int}}}
   {Q_{\mathrm{A}}^{\mathrm{tra}} \, Q_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{tra}}}
   \exp\!\left(\frac{E_{\mathrm{A}} + E_{\mathrm{B}}}{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}\right)

For a general reaction, the equilibrium constant is computed as (see also
:ref:`thermo_keq`):

.. math::

   \ln K_{\mathrm{eq}} = \sum_c \nu_c
   \left[\ln Q_c^{\mathrm{tra}} + \ln Q_c^{\mathrm{int}}
   + \ln m_c - \frac{e_{f,c}}{R_c T}\right]
   + \Delta\nu \ln\!\left(\frac{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}{p}\right)

**Alternative: Gibbs free energy path.** When NASA polynomials are enabled
[Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.3):

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_Keq_gibbs_kin

   K_{\mathrm{eq}} = \exp\!\left(
   -\frac{\Delta G^0(T)}{R_u T} \right)

Both paths yield the same equilibrium constant; see :ref:`ex07_thermo` for
numerical verification.

.. note::

   Enforcing microreversibility ensures **thermodynamic consistency**: the
   system always relaxes toward the correct equilibrium. Using independently
   fitted forward and backward rates can violate the second law and produce
   spurious behavior.

Source: ``src/kinetics/kinetics_rate.F90``.

Reaction Process Types
======================

PLATO implements 16 gas-phase reaction process types. Each type specifies the
collision partners, the governing temperature, and the stoichiometry:

.. list-table::
   :widths: 5 12 35 10
   :header-rows: 1

   * - ID
     - Symbol
     - Process
     - Temperature
   * - 1
     - ``Dh_cat_sts``
     - Dissociation by heavy impact (StS): AB(i) + M :math:`\rightleftharpoons` A(j) + B(k) + M
     - :math:`T_h`
   * - 2
     - ``Dh_cat_mt``
     - Dissociation by heavy impact (MT): same, Park :math:`T_{T_v}`
     - :math:`T_{T_v}`
   * - 3
     - ``Dh``
     - Dissociation by specific heavy partner: AB(i) + M(j) :math:`\rightleftharpoons` A(k) + B(l) + M(j')
     - :math:`T_h`
   * - 4
     - ``De``
     - Dissociation by electron impact: AB(i) + e\ :sup:`-` :math:`\rightleftharpoons` A(j) + B(k) + e\ :sup:`-`
     - :math:`T_e`
   * - 5
     - ``PD``
     - Predissociation (tunneling): AB(i) :math:`\to` A(j) + B(k)
     - :math:`T_h`
   * - 6
     - ``EXh``
     - Excitation by heavy impact: A(i) + M :math:`\rightleftharpoons` A*(j) + M
     - :math:`T_h`
   * - 7
     - ``EXC``
     - Exchange / combined excitation: A(i) + B(j) :math:`\rightleftharpoons` C(k) + D(l)
     - :math:`T_h`
   * - 8
     - ``EXe``
     - Excitation by electron impact: A(i) + e\ :sup:`-` :math:`\rightleftharpoons` A*(j) + e\ :sup:`-`
     - :math:`T_e`
   * - 9
     - ``Ie``
     - Ionization by electron: A(i) + e\ :sup:`-` :math:`\rightleftharpoons` A\ :sup:`+`\(j) + 2e\ :sup:`-`
     - :math:`T_e`
   * - 10
     - ``Ih``
     - Ionization by heavy: A(i) + B(j) :math:`\rightleftharpoons` C(k) + D(l) + e\ :sup:`-`
     - :math:`T_h`
   * - 11
     - ``AI``
     - Associative ionization: A(i) + B(j) :math:`\rightleftharpoons` AB\ :sup:`+`\(k) + e\ :sup:`-`
     - :math:`T_h`
   * - 12
     - ``PI``
     - Photoionization
     - ---
   * - 13
     - ``DIe``
     - Dissociative ionization: AB(i) + e\ :sup:`-` :math:`\rightleftharpoons` B(j) + C(k) + 2e\ :sup:`-`
     - :math:`T_e`
   * - 14
     - ``Rh_cat``
     - Particle/charge exchange (Zel'dovich): A(i) + B(j) + M :math:`\rightleftharpoons` AB(k) + M
     - :math:`T_h`
   * - 15
     - ``line_AE``
     - Line absorption/emission: A(i) + h\ :math:`\nu` :math:`\rightleftharpoons` A*(j)
     - ---
   * - 16
     - ``RR``
     - Radiative recombination: A\ :sup:`+`\(i) + e\ :sup:`-` :math:`\to` A(j) + h\ :math:`\nu`
     - :math:`T_e`


.. _kinetics_source:

Mass Production Rates
=====================

The mass production rate of species *s* is:

.. math::
   :label: eq_omega_s

   \dot{\omega}_s = m_s \sum_{r=1}^{N_r}
   \left( \nu_{s,r}^{\prime\prime} - \nu_{s,r}^{\prime} \right)
   \left(
   k_{f,r} \prod_{i} n_i^{\nu_{i,r}^{\prime}}
   - k_{b,r} \prod_{j} n_j^{\nu_{j,r}^{\prime\prime}}
   \right)

where :math:`\nu^{\prime}` and :math:`\nu^{\prime\prime}` are the reactant
and product stoichiometric coefficients, and :math:`m_s` converts from
number to mass rates.

For catalyzed reactions (types 1, 2, 6, 14) involving a third body M, the
production term includes the total number density of collision partners:

.. math::

   \dot{n}_{\mathrm{prod}} = n_M \left(
   n_i \, k_f - k_b \, n_j \, n_k \right)

where :math:`n_M = \sum_s n_s` (or a weighted sum with specified efficiencies).


Source Term Computation Pipeline
---------------------------------

The source term evaluation in ``get_source()`` proceeds as follows:

1. Convert partial densities to number densities: :math:`n_i = \rho_i / m_i`
2. Clamp temperatures (enforce positivity)
3. Compute forward rate coefficients :math:`k_f` via :eq:`th_eq_arrhenius`
4. Compute backward rate coefficients :math:`k_b` via :eq:`eq_kb`
5. Loop over 16 reaction categories, accumulate production terms
6. Convert to mass rates: :math:`\dot{\omega}_s = m_s \, \dot{n}_s`
7. Add energy exchange terms (:ref:`kinetics_transfer`)

**Output:** ``omega(1:nb_sp)`` = mass production rates [kg/m\ :sup:`3`/s],
``omega(nb_sp+nb_dim+1:...)`` = energy exchange rates [W/m\ :sup:`3`].

Source: ``src/kinetics/kinetics_source_mass.F90``.


.. _kinetics_transfer:

Energy Exchange Terms
=====================

PLATO computes six energy transfer mechanisms that couple the different
temperature equations.


Vibration--Translation (VT) Relaxation
---------------------------------------

The Landau--Teller model describes VT relaxation as exponential
approach to translational equilibrium [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs. 3.40--3.41):

.. math::
   :label: eq_omega_vt

   \Omega_{\mathrm{VT}} = \sum_{m \in \mathrm{mol}}
   \rho_m \frac{e_m^{\mathrm{vib}}(T_h) - e_m^{\mathrm{vib}}(T_v)}
   {\tau_{\mathrm{VT},m}}

Note the sign convention: :math:`\Omega_{\mathrm{VT}} > 0` when
:math:`T_h > T_v`, meaning energy flows **from** translation **to**
vibration.

The mixture-averaged relaxation time uses Millikan--White mixing:

.. math::

   p \, \tau_{\mathrm{VT},m} = \left(\sum_h \frac{X_h}{p \, \tau_{h\text{-}m}}\right)^{-1}

where each pair relaxation time :math:`\tau_{h\text{-}m}` is given by the
**Millikan--White** correlation [MillikanWhite1963]_:

.. math::

   p \, \tau_{h\text{-}m} = \exp\!\left[
   a \left(T^{-1/3} - b\right) - 18.42 \right] \quad [\mathrm{atm \cdot s}]

with :math:`a = 1.16 \times 10^{-3} \mu_{hm}^{1/2} \theta_{\mathrm{vib}}^{4/3}`
and :math:`b = 0.015 \mu_{hm}^{1/4}`. A **Park high-temperature correction**
[Park1989]_ is added at :math:`T > 8000` K to prevent unphysical divergence.

Source: ``src/kinetics/transfer_vt.F90``.


Vibration--Electron (VE) Exchange
----------------------------------

For electron-impact vibrational excitation (primarily N\ :sub:`2`--e\ :sup:`-`):

.. math::
   :label: eq_omega_ve

   \Omega_{\mathrm{VE}} = \sum_{m}
   \frac{\rho_m \left[ e_m^{\mathrm{vib}}(T_e) - e_m^{\mathrm{vib}}(T_v) \right]}
   {\tau_{\mathrm{VE},m}}

The relaxation time :math:`\tau_{\mathrm{VE}}` is computed from piecewise
polynomial fits in :math:`\log_{10} T_e`.

Source: ``src/kinetics/transfer_ve.F90``.


Translation--Electron (TE) Elastic Exchange
--------------------------------------------

Elastic collisions between electrons and heavy particles:

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_omega_te

   \Omega_{\mathrm{TE}} = \frac{3}{2} k_{\mathrm{B}} \, n_e
   \left(T_h - T_e\right) \nu_{eh}

where :math:`\nu_{eh}` is the electron--heavy particle collision frequency.

Source: ``src/kinetics/transfer_te.F90``.


Chemistry--Vibration (CV) Coupling
-----------------------------------

The vibrational energy added or removed by chemical reactions:

.. math::
   :label: eq_omega_cv

   \Omega_{\mathrm{CV}} = \sum_{m \in \mathrm{mol}} \sum_r
   \dot{\omega}_{m,r} \, e_m^{\mathrm{vib}}(T_v)

This is evaluated *after* the chemical source terms are computed.

Source: ``src/kinetics/transfer_cv.F90``.


Chemistry--Electronic (CEL) Coupling
--------------------------------------

The electronic energy carried by chemical reactions:

.. math::
   :label: eq_omega_cel

   \Omega_{\mathrm{CEL}} = \sum_s \sum_r
   \dot{\omega}_{s,r} \, e_s^{\mathrm{el}}(T_e)

Source: ``src/kinetics/transfer_cel.F90``.


Free-Free Bremsstrahlung (FF)
------------------------------

Energy loss by electron Bremsstrahlung radiation (Kramers formula with
Gaunt factor = 1):

.. math::
   :label: eq_omega_ff

   \Omega_{\mathrm{ff}} = C_{\mathrm{ff}}
   \left(\sum_{c \in \mathrm{ions}} Z_c \, n_c\right)
   n_e \sqrt{T_e}

where :math:`Z_c` is the charge number and :math:`C_{\mathrm{ff}}` is a
constant from the Kramers cross-section.

Source: ``src/kinetics/transfer_rad_ff.F90``.


.. _kinetics_jacobian:

Analytical Jacobian
===================

PLATO provides the full analytical Jacobian
:math:`\partial(\dot{\omega}, \Omega) / \partial(\rho_i, T_k)` for implicit
time integration. The Jacobian matrix has dimensions:

.. math::

   \mathbf{J} \in \mathbb{R}^{(n_s + n_{\mathrm{dim}} + n_T)
   \times (n_s + n_{\mathrm{dim}} + n_T)}

where :math:`n_s` is the number of species, :math:`n_{\mathrm{dim}}` the
spatial dimension, and :math:`n_T` the number of temperatures.

**Column ordering:**

- Columns :math:`1` to :math:`n_s`: partial densities :math:`\rho_i`
- Columns :math:`n_s+1` to :math:`n_s+n_{\mathrm{dim}}`: spatial coordinates
- Remaining columns: temperatures :math:`T_h, T_v, T_e, \ldots`

**Key derivative terms** (example for catalyzed dissociation, type 1):

.. math::

   \frac{\partial \dot{n}}{\partial n_i} = n_M \, k_f, \qquad
   \frac{\partial \dot{n}}{\partial n_j} = -k_b \, n_M \, n_k, \qquad
   \frac{\partial \dot{n}}{\partial T} =
   \left(\frac{n}{T} + \frac{\theta_a}{T^2}\right) n_M \left(
   n_i \, k_f - k_b \, n_j \, n_k \right)

The chain rule relates partial-density derivatives to number-density
derivatives:

.. math::

   \frac{\partial}{\partial \rho_i} = \frac{1}{m_i}
   \frac{\partial}{\partial n_i}

For the Park activation temperature :eq:`eq_park_T`, additional chain-rule
terms propagate :math:`\partial T_{T_v}/\partial T_h` and
:math:`\partial T_{T_v}/\partial T_v` into the Jacobian.

Energy transfer Jacobians (VT, VE, TE, CV, CEL, FF) are included in the
same matrix.

A **mass-only** Jacobian variant (:math:`n_s \times n_s`) is available for
simplified time integration.

Source: ``src/kinetics/kinetics_source_jac.F90``.


.. _kinetics_cg:

Coarse-Grained Kinetics
========================

For coarse-grained (CG) models, rate coefficients come from pre-tabulated
binary lookup tables with **bicubic interpolation** rather than Arrhenius
expressions. The Jacobian is computed **numerically** by finite differences
(not analytically). Tables reside in ``database/kinetics/{ng}g/``.

Source: ``src/kinetics/kinetics_group.F90``.
