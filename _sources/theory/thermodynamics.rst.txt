.. _theory_thermo:

=============================
Thermodynamic Properties
=============================

This chapter describes how PLATO computes per-species and mixture
thermodynamic properties from statistical mechanics (the default path) and
from NASA 9-coefficient polynomials (the optional path). The implementation
resides in ``src/thermo/`` (~28 source files).

The presentation follows [Munafo_thesis]_ (Chapter 1) for the partition
function framework, [Magin_thesis]_ (Chapter 2) for entropy and Gibbs free
energy, and [MunafoPanesi2025]_ for the overall formulation.


Statistical Mechanics Foundation
================================

The central idea of statistical thermodynamics is that **macroscopic
properties emerge from the partition function** :math:`Q`, which counts
the number of accessible microstates weighted by their Boltzmann probability.
Once :math:`Q` is known, all thermodynamic quantities follow by differentiation.

For an ideal gas, the energy modes are assumed to be **separable**:
translational motion is classical (continuous), while internal modes
(rotation, vibration, electronic excitation) are quantized. This allows the
total partition function to factorize, and each factor can be evaluated at
the temperature governing that mode.

.. figure:: figures/energy_levels_partition.png
   :width: 100%
   :align: center

   Left: Energy level structure of a diatomic molecule showing the separation
   of scales between electronic (:math:`\sim 10^4` K), vibrational
   (:math:`\sim 10^3` K), rotational (:math:`\sim 1` K), and translational
   (continuous) modes. Center: The corresponding partition function
   factorization. Right: How all thermodynamic properties derive from
   :math:`Q` by differentiation.


.. _thermo_partition:

Partition Functions
===================

The internal partition function of species *i* factorizes under the assumption
of separable energy modes [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 1.35):

.. math::
   :label: eq_Q_total

   Q_i^{\mathrm{int}} = Q_i^{\mathrm{rot}} \; Q_i^{\mathrm{vib}} \;
   Q_i^{\mathrm{el}}

The translational partition function :math:`Q_i^{\mathrm{tra}}` is treated
separately because it depends on the thermodynamic state (pressure or
density), while the internal partition function depends only on temperature.

.. note::

   PLATO uses the symbol :math:`Q` for partition functions (variable name
   ``Q`` in the source code). The Munafò thesis [Munafo_thesis]_ uses
   :math:`Z`, but the notation is otherwise identical.


Translational Partition Function
--------------------------------

For a 3D ideal gas of species *i* at temperature *T* and pressure *p*
[Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 1.35):

.. math::
   :label: eq_Q_tra

   Q_i^{\mathrm{tra}}(T) = \left(\frac{2 \pi m_i k_{\mathrm{B}} T}{h^2}
   \right)^{3/2}

This is the partition function **per unit volume**. The factor
:math:`k_{\mathrm{B}} T / p` converts to a per-particle partition function at
pressure *p* when needed (e.g., in the Sackur--Tetrode equation and the
equilibrium constant).

The translational partition function carries the **mass dependence**: heavier
species have more translational states available at a given temperature
(larger :math:`Q^{\mathrm{tra}}`), because heavier particles have smaller
de Broglie wavelengths :math:`\Lambda_i = h / \sqrt{2 \pi m_i k_{\mathrm{B}} T}`.

Free electrons are treated identically but evaluated at :math:`T_e` rather
than :math:`T_h`, with mass :math:`m_e`.


Rotational Partition Function (Rigid Rotor)
-------------------------------------------

For a **linear molecule** (atomicity > 1, single moment of inertia *I*),
in the high-temperature limit where :math:`T \gg \theta_{\mathrm{rot}}`
[Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 3.53):

.. math::
   :label: eq_Q_rot_lin

   Q_i^{\mathrm{rot}}(T) = \frac{T}{\sigma_i \, \theta_{\mathrm{rot},i}}

where:

- :math:`\theta_{\mathrm{rot},i} = h^2 / (8 \pi^2 I_i k_{\mathrm{B}})` is
  the characteristic rotational temperature (typically ~2 K for diatomics,
  so the high-*T* limit is always valid in hypersonic flows)
- :math:`\sigma_i` is the symmetry number (2 for homonuclear molecules like
  N\ :sub:`2`, O\ :sub:`2`; 1 for heteronuclear molecules like NO)

For a **nonlinear molecule** with three principal moments of inertia:

.. math::
   :label: eq_Q_rot_nonlin

   Q_i^{\mathrm{rot}}(T) = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{\sigma_i}
   \left( \frac{T^3}{\theta_{\mathrm{rot},a} \,
   \theta_{\mathrm{rot},b} \, \theta_{\mathrm{rot},c}} \right)^{1/2}

**Atoms** (atomicity = 1) have no rotational degrees of freedom:
:math:`Q^{\mathrm{rot}} = 1`.

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_utils.F90``, lines 55--76.


Vibrational Partition Function (Harmonic Oscillator)
----------------------------------------------------

For a molecule with :math:`n_{\mathrm{vib}}` vibrational modes, each
modeled as an independent quantum harmonic oscillator:

.. math::
   :label: eq_Q_vib

   Q_i^{\mathrm{vib}}(T) = \prod_{j=1}^{n_{\mathrm{vib}}}
   \frac{1}{1 - \exp(-\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j} / T)}

where :math:`\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}` is the characteristic vibrational
temperature of mode *j* (e.g., :math:`\theta_{\mathrm{vib}} = 3393` K for
N\ :sub:`2`, :math:`\theta_{\mathrm{vib}} = 2273` K for O\ :sub:`2`).

The energy zero is at the ground vibrational state (:math:`v = 0`). The
vibrational partition function approaches 1 as :math:`T \to 0` (only the
ground state is populated) and grows linearly as :math:`T / \theta_{\mathrm{vib}}`
at high temperatures (classical limit).

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_utils.F90``, lines 118--129.


Electronic Partition Function (Boltzmann)
-----------------------------------------

The electronic partition function is a direct sum over discrete energy levels:

.. math::
   :label: eq_Q_el

   Q_i^{\mathrm{el}}(T) = \sum_{k=1}^{N_{\mathrm{el}}}
   g_k \exp\!\left(-\frac{\varepsilon_k}{T}\right)

where :math:`g_k` is the degeneracy and :math:`\varepsilon_k` is the energy
of the *k*-th electronic level, expressed in Kelvin
(:math:`\varepsilon_k = E_k / k_{\mathrm{B}}`). Level data (energies and
degeneracies) are read from the species files in ``database/thermo/``.

At low temperatures, only the ground state contributes:
:math:`Q^{\mathrm{el}} \approx g_1`. At high temperatures, excited states
become populated and :math:`Q^{\mathrm{el}}` increases.

.. warning::

   The electronic partition function formally diverges for atoms because the
   number of bound states is infinite. In practice, PLATO truncates the
   sum at a finite number of levels from the database. When Debye--Hückel
   corrections are active (see :ref:`equil_debye`), the truncation is
   determined by Griem's criterion based on the local plasma conditions.

For **state-to-state** (StS) pseudo-species, each level is treated as an
independent species with :math:`Q^{\mathrm{el}} = g_k` (just the
degeneracy) and zero internal energy above its own reference.

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_utils.F90``, lines 155--175.


.. _thermo_model_dispatch:

Model Dispatch Mechanism
========================

PLATO uses a **MODEL** parameter (set per species in the database files) to
select which partition function and thermodynamic routines are called at
runtime. This is implemented via **procedure pointer arrays** assigned during
initialization in ``src/thermo/thermo_initialize.F90`` (lines 1320--1427):

.. list-table::
   :widths: 18 50 14
   :header-rows: 1

   * - MODEL
     - Description
     - Target
   * - ``RR_HO_EL``
     - Rigid rotor + harmonic oscillator + electronic (default)
     - Molecules
   * - ``EL``
     - Electronic Boltzmann only
     - Atoms
   * - ``RR``
     - Rigid rotor only
     - Special
   * - ``HO``
     - Harmonic oscillator only
     - Special
   * - ``RR_HO``
     - Rigid rotor + harmonic oscillator (no electronic)
     - Molecules
   * - ``NONE`` / ``STS``
     - State-to-state (:math:`Q = g`, :math:`e^{\mathrm{int}} = 0`)
     - Any
   * - ``GROUP`` / ``RR_GROUP``
     - Coarse-grained (table interpolation)
     - Molecules

For example, the pointer assignment for the ``RR_HO_EL`` model assigns:

- Partition function: ``rr_ho_el_Q``
- Energy: ``rr_ho_el_e``
- Entropy: ``rr_ho_el_s``
- Gibbs: ``rr_ho_el_g``

This polymorphic dispatch avoids runtime branching in inner loops: the
function pointer is resolved once at initialization, and all subsequent calls
go directly to the correct implementation.


.. _thermo_derivation:

From Partition Functions to Thermodynamic Properties
====================================================

This section shows how all thermodynamic properties are **derived** from the
partition function. This is the fundamental connection between microscopic
(quantum) and macroscopic (continuum) descriptions.

The key relation is [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs. 1.42--1.43):

.. math::
   :label: eq_e_from_Q

   e_i^{\mathrm{mode}}(T) = \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}} T^2}{m_i}
   \frac{\partial \ln Q_i^{\mathrm{mode}}}{\partial T}

This formula states that the specific energy of any mode equals
:math:`k_{\mathrm{B}} T^2 / m_i` times the logarithmic temperature derivative
of the corresponding partition function. It is the bridge between statistical
mechanics and thermodynamics.

**Derivation sketch.** Consider a canonical ensemble of :math:`N_i` particles
of species *i* in volume *V*. The Helmholtz free energy is
:math:`F = -N_i k_{\mathrm{B}} T \ln Q_i`. The internal energy is
:math:`U = -\partial \ln Q_i / \partial (1/(k_{\mathrm{B}} T))
= k_{\mathrm{B}} T^2 \, \partial \ln Q_i / \partial T`. Dividing by mass
:math:`N_i m_i` gives the specific energy :eq:`eq_e_from_Q`.

Similarly, the specific heat follows by differentiation:

.. math::
   :label: eq_cv_from_Q

   c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{mode}} = \frac{\partial e_i^{\mathrm{mode}}}{\partial T}

The entropy per unit mass of a mode is:

.. math::
   :label: eq_s_from_Q

   s_i^{\mathrm{mode}} = \frac{e_i^{\mathrm{mode}}}{T}
   + \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}}}{m_i} \ln Q_i^{\mathrm{mode}}

And the Gibbs free energy per unit mass:

.. math::
   :label: eq_g_from_Q

   g_i^{\mathrm{mode}} = -\frac{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}{m_i}
   \ln Q_i^{\mathrm{mode}}

(plus the :math:`pV = R_i T` term for translation).

.. note::

   In the source code (``src/thermo/thermo_utils.F90``), the electronic
   energy is computed **directly** from the Boltzmann-weighted sum
   :math:`e = \sum_k \varepsilon_k g_k \exp(-\varepsilon_k/T) / Q`
   rather than via the logarithmic derivative :eq:`eq_e_from_Q`. Both
   approaches are mathematically equivalent; the direct sum is numerically
   more convenient when the level energies are tabulated.


.. _thermo_energy:

Internal Energy
===============

Applying :eq:`eq_e_from_Q` to each partition function, the total specific
internal energy of species *i* decomposes as [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eqs.
1.42--1.44):

.. math::
   :label: eq_energy_total

   e_i = e_i^{\mathrm{tra}} + e_i^{\mathrm{rot}} + e_i^{\mathrm{vib}}
   + e_i^{\mathrm{el}} + e_{f,i}

where :math:`e_{f,i}` is the formation energy at 0 K (see
:ref:`thermo_formation`).


Translational Energy
--------------------

From :math:`\ln Q^{\mathrm{tra}} = \tfrac{3}{2} \ln T + \mathrm{const}`, we
get :math:`\partial \ln Q^{\mathrm{tra}} / \partial T = 3/(2T)`, so:

.. math::
   :label: eq_e_tra

   e_i^{\mathrm{tra}} = \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}} T^2}{m_i} \cdot \frac{3}{2T}
   = \frac{3}{2} \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}}}{m_i} T = \frac{3}{2} R_i \, T_h

This is the classical equipartition result: :math:`\tfrac{1}{2} k_{\mathrm{B}} T`
per translational degree of freedom, three degrees per particle.

For free electrons:

.. math::
   :label: eq_e_tra_e

   e_e^{\mathrm{tra}} = \frac{3}{2} \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}}}{m_e} T_e


Rotational Energy
-----------------

From :math:`\ln Q^{\mathrm{rot}} = \ln T - \ln(\sigma \theta_{\mathrm{rot}})`
for a linear molecule, :math:`\partial \ln Q^{\mathrm{rot}} / \partial T = 1/T`:

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_e_rot

   e_i^{\mathrm{rot}} = R_i \, T_r \qquad \text{(linear molecule)}

For a nonlinear molecule (:math:`\ln Q^{\mathrm{rot}} \propto \tfrac{3}{2} \ln T`):

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_e_rot_nonlin

   e_i^{\mathrm{rot}} = \frac{3}{2} R_i \, T_r \qquad \text{(nonlinear molecule)}

In the standard two-temperature model, :math:`T_r = T_h` (rotation
equilibrates with translation on fast timescales).


Vibrational Energy
------------------

From the harmonic oscillator partition function :eq:`eq_Q_vib`:

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_e_vib

   e_i^{\mathrm{vib}} = R_i \sum_{j=1}^{n_{\mathrm{vib}}}
   \frac{\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}}{\exp(\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j} / T_v) - 1}

This is the **Planck distribution** for each vibrational mode. At low
temperature (:math:`T_v \ll \theta_{\mathrm{vib}}`), the mode is "frozen"
with exponentially small energy. At high temperature
(:math:`T_v \gg \theta_{\mathrm{vib}}`), the energy approaches the classical
limit :math:`R_i T_v` per mode.


Electronic Energy
-----------------

The mean electronic energy from the Boltzmann distribution:

.. math::
   :label: eq_e_el

   e_i^{\mathrm{el}} = \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}}}{m_i}
   \frac{\sum_{k=1}^{N_{\mathrm{el}}} \varepsilon_k \, g_k
   \exp(-\varepsilon_k / T_e)}{Q_i^{\mathrm{el}}(T_e)}

This is a thermal average: each level's energy is weighted by its Boltzmann
population. At low temperatures, only the ground state is populated and
:math:`e^{\mathrm{el}} \approx 0`. The electronic energy becomes significant
when :math:`T_e` is comparable to the first excited state energy
(typically thousands of Kelvin).

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_prop_energy.F90``,
``src/thermo/thermo_utils.F90``.


Energy Densities
----------------

The conserved energy densities used in the governing equations are:

.. math::
   :label: eq_rhoe_h

   \rho e_h = \sum_{i \neq e} \rho_i
   \left[ \frac{3}{2} R_i T_h + e_i^{\mathrm{int}} + e_{f,i} \right]

.. math::
   :label: eq_rhoe_e

   \rho e_e = \rho_e \frac{3}{2} \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}}}{m_e} T_e

where the sum in :eq:`eq_rhoe_h` runs over heavy particles only and
:math:`e_i^{\mathrm{int}} = e_i^{\mathrm{rot}} + e_i^{\mathrm{vib}} + e_i^{\mathrm{el}}`.


.. _thermo_enthalpy:

Enthalpy
========

The specific enthalpy of species *i* includes the :math:`pV` work term.
For an ideal gas, :math:`pV = n k_{\mathrm{B}} T` per particle, so per unit
mass [Munafo_thesis]_ (text after Eq. 1.44):

.. math::
   :label: eq_enthalpy

   h_i = e_i + \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}}}{m_i} T = e_i + R_i \, T

Explicitly, for heavy particles:

.. math::
   :label: eq_h_heavy

   h_i = \frac{5}{2} R_i \, T_h + e_i^{\mathrm{rot}} + e_i^{\mathrm{vib}}
   + e_i^{\mathrm{el}} + e_{f,i}

The :math:`5/2` comes from :math:`3/2` (translational :math:`c_v`) plus
:math:`1` (the :math:`pV` term). This is also called the **translational
enthalpy**: :math:`h_i^{\mathrm{tra}} = \tfrac{5}{2} R_i T` [Magin_thesis]_
(Eq. 2.4).

For free electrons:

.. math::
   :label: eq_h_electron

   h_e = \frac{5}{2} \frac{k_{\mathrm{B}}}{m_e} T_e

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_prop_enthalpy.F90``.


.. _thermo_cv_cp:

Specific Heats
==============

The specific heat at constant volume is the temperature derivative of the
energy [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 1.45):

.. math::

   c_{v,i} = \frac{\partial e_i}{\partial T}

and the specific heat at constant pressure (for an ideal gas):

.. math::

   c_{p,i} = c_{v,i} + R_i

Note that the internal modes contribute equally to :math:`c_v` and :math:`c_p`
because they involve no :math:`pV` work: :math:`c_{p,i}^{\mathrm{int}} = c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{int}}`
[Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 1.47).


Specific Heat at Constant Volume
---------------------------------

**Translational** (equipartition, exact):

.. math::

   c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{tra}} = \frac{3}{2} R_i

**Rotational** (rigid-rotor, fully excited):

.. math::

   c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{rot}} = \begin{cases}
   R_i & \text{linear molecule} \\[4pt]
   \tfrac{3}{2} R_i & \text{nonlinear molecule}
   \end{cases}

**Vibrational** (harmonic oscillator):

.. math::
   :label: eq_cv_vib

   c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{vib}} = R_i \sum_{j=1}^{n_{\mathrm{vib}}}
   \frac{(\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}/T)^2 \exp(\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}/T)}
   {[\exp(\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}/T) - 1]^2}

This is the **Einstein function**: it vanishes exponentially as
:math:`T \to 0` and approaches :math:`R_i` per mode as
:math:`T \to \infty`.

**Electronic** (Boltzmann, variance of the energy distribution):

.. math::
   :label: eq_cv_el

   c_{v,i}^{\mathrm{el}} = \frac{R_i}{T^2}
   \left[
   \frac{\sum_k \varepsilon_k^2 g_k e^{-\varepsilon_k/T}}{Q_i^{\mathrm{el}}}
   - \left(\frac{\sum_k \varepsilon_k g_k e^{-\varepsilon_k/T}}
   {Q_i^{\mathrm{el}}}\right)^{\!2}
   \right]

This is :math:`\langle \varepsilon^2 \rangle - \langle \varepsilon \rangle^2`
divided by :math:`T^2`: the electronic specific heat is proportional to the
**variance** of the electronic energy distribution. It peaks when
:math:`T \sim \varepsilon_1` (the first excitation energy) and vanishes
both at low *T* (one state occupied) and high *T* (all states equally
populated).


Specific Heat at Constant Pressure
------------------------------------

.. math::

   c_{p,i} = c_{v,i} + R_i

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_prop_cv.F90``, ``src/thermo/thermo_prop_cp.F90``.


.. _thermo_entropy:

Entropy
=======

The entropy is derived from the partition function via :eq:`eq_s_from_Q`.
The specific entropy decomposes into modal contributions [Magin_thesis]_
(Eqs. 2.4--2.7):

.. math::
   :label: eq_entropy_total

   s_i = s_i^{\mathrm{tra}} + s_i^{\mathrm{rot}} + s_i^{\mathrm{vib}}
   + s_i^{\mathrm{el}}


Translational Entropy (Sackur--Tetrode)
-----------------------------------------

The translational entropy includes the pressure dependence because the
translational partition function depends on the available volume per particle.
From :eq:`eq_s_from_Q` applied to :eq:`eq_Q_tra` [Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.4c):

.. math::
   :label: eq_sackur_tetrode

   s_i^{\mathrm{tra}} = R_i \left[ \frac{5}{2} + \frac{3}{2}
   \ln\!\left(\frac{2\pi m_i k_{\mathrm{B}} T}{h^2}\right)
   + \ln\!\left(\frac{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}{p}\right) \right]

This is the classical **Sackur--Tetrode equation** expressed per unit mass.
As expected from the third law of thermodynamics, :math:`s^{\mathrm{tra}} \to -\infty`
as :math:`T \to 0` (the quantum regime where the classical partition function
breaks down).


Rotational Entropy
-------------------

For a linear molecule, from :eq:`eq_s_from_Q` applied to :eq:`eq_Q_rot_lin`:

.. math::
   :label: eq_s_rot_lin

   s_i^{\mathrm{rot}} = R_i \left[ 1 + \ln\!\left(
   \frac{T}{\sigma_i \, \theta_{\mathrm{rot},i}} \right) \right]

For a nonlinear molecule:

.. math::
   :label: eq_s_rot_nonlin

   s_i^{\mathrm{rot}} = R_i \left[ \frac{3}{2}
   + \frac{3}{2} \ln\!\left(\frac{T}{\theta_{\mathrm{rot},i}}\right)
   - \ln \sigma_i \right]

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_function.F90``, line 1614.


Vibrational Entropy
--------------------

From :eq:`eq_s_from_Q` applied to :eq:`eq_Q_vib` [Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.7b):

.. math::
   :label: eq_s_vib

   s_i^{\mathrm{vib}} = R_i \sum_{j=1}^{n_{\mathrm{vib}}}
   \left[
   \frac{\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}/T}{\exp(\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}/T) - 1}
   - \ln\!\left(1 - \exp(-\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}/T)\right)
   \right]

The first term is :math:`e^{\mathrm{vib}} / (R_i T)` and the second is
:math:`\ln Q^{\mathrm{vib}}`.


Electronic Entropy
-------------------

.. math::
   :label: eq_s_el

   s_i^{\mathrm{el}} = R_i \left[
   \ln Q_i^{\mathrm{el}} +
   \frac{1}{T_e}
   \frac{\sum_k \varepsilon_k g_k \exp(-\varepsilon_k/T_e)}
   {Q_i^{\mathrm{el}}}
   \right]

This is :eq:`eq_s_from_Q` with the electronic partition function:
:math:`s = e/T + (k_{\mathrm{B}}/m_i) \ln Q` [Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.5b).

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_function.F90``, line 1699.


Entropy of Mixing
------------------

The mixture specific entropy includes an ideal-gas mixing term
[Magin_thesis]_ (p. 35):

.. math::
   :label: eq_s_mix

   s_{\mathrm{mix}} = \sum_i y_i \, s_i
   - \sum_i y_i \, R_i \ln X_i

The :math:`-R_i \ln X_i` term is always positive (since :math:`X_i < 1`),
reflecting the entropy increase upon mixing.

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_prop_entropy.F90``.


.. _thermo_gibbs:

Gibbs Free Energy
=================

The specific Gibbs free energy is the key quantity for chemical equilibrium
[Magin_thesis]_ (p. 35):

.. math::
   :label: eq_gibbs

   g_i = h_i - T \, s_i

From :eq:`eq_g_from_Q`, each modal contribution is
:math:`g^{\mathrm{mode}} = -R_i T \ln Q^{\mathrm{mode}}` (with the
translational term including the :math:`pV` contribution).

Translational:

.. math::
   :label: eq_g_tra

   g_i^{\mathrm{tra}} = R_i T \left[ -\frac{5}{2} \ln T
   - \ln\!\left(\frac{(2\pi m_i k_{\mathrm{B}})^{3/2}}{h^3}\right)
   - \ln k_{\mathrm{B}} + \ln p \right]

Rotational (linear molecule):

.. math::

   g_i^{\mathrm{rot}} = -R_i T \ln\!\left(
   \frac{T}{\sigma_i \, \theta_{\mathrm{rot},i}} \right)

Vibrational:

.. math::

   g_i^{\mathrm{vib}} = R_i T \sum_{j=1}^{n_{\mathrm{vib}}}
   \ln\!\left(1 - \exp(-\theta_{\mathrm{vib},j}/T)\right)

Electronic:

.. math::

   g_i^{\mathrm{el}} = -R_i T \ln Q_i^{\mathrm{el}}

The Gibbs free energy is central to chemical equilibrium because **a
reaction proceeds spontaneously in the direction that decreases the total
Gibbs energy**. At equilibrium, :math:`\sum_i \nu_i g_i = 0` (the law of
mass action; see :ref:`theory_equilibrium`).

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_prop_gibbs.F90``.


.. _thermo_helmholtz:

Helmholtz Free Energy
=====================

The specific Helmholtz free energy is:

.. math::
   :label: eq_helmholtz

   f_i = e_i - T \, s_i = g_i - R_i \, T

For free electrons: :math:`f_e = g_e - (k_{\mathrm{B}}/m_e) T_e`.

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_prop_gibbs.F90``, lines 126--147.


.. _thermo_keq:

Equilibrium Constant from Partition Functions
=============================================

The equilibrium constant connects microscopic (partition function) and
macroscopic (reaction rate) descriptions. Its derivation from partition
functions is one of the central results in PLATO.

Consider a generic dissociation reaction:

.. math::

   \mathrm{AB}(i) + \mathrm{M} \rightleftharpoons \mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B} + \mathrm{M}

**Microscopic microreversibility** [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.80) relates the
state-specific forward and backward rate coefficients through the principle
of detailed balance. At equilibrium, the forward and backward rates must be
equal for every microscopic channel:

.. math::
   :label: eq_micro_rev

   \frac{k_i^{\mathrm{rec}}(T)}{k_i^{\mathrm{dis}}(T)}
   = \frac{g_i \, Q_{\mathrm{AB}}^{\mathrm{tra}}(T)}
   {\left[g_{\mathrm{A}} \, Q_{\mathrm{A}}^{\mathrm{tra}}(T)\right]
   \left[g_{\mathrm{B}} \, Q_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{tra}}(T)\right]}
   \exp\!\left(\frac{E_{\mathrm{A}} + E_{\mathrm{B}} - E_i}{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}\right)

where :math:`g_i` is the degeneracy of level *i*, :math:`E_i` its energy,
and :math:`E_{\mathrm{A}}, E_{\mathrm{B}}` are the atomic formation
energies.

**Macroscopic equilibrium constant.** Summing over all internal levels with
Boltzmann weights [Munafo_thesis]_ (Eq. 3.48):

.. math::
   :label: eq_Keq_partition

   K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T) = \frac{Q_{\mathrm{AB}}^{\mathrm{tra}}(T) \;
   Q_{\mathrm{AB}}^{\mathrm{int}}(T)}
   {Q_{\mathrm{A}}^{\mathrm{tra}}(T) \; Q_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{tra}}(T)}
   \exp\!\left(\frac{E_{\mathrm{A}} + E_{\mathrm{B}}}{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}\right)

This is the **fundamental result**: the equilibrium constant is a ratio of
partition functions, multiplied by a Boltzmann factor that accounts for the
energy difference between products and reactants.

For a general reaction :math:`\sum_i \nu_i A_i = 0` (with :math:`\nu_i > 0`
for products, :math:`\nu_i < 0` for reactants), the implementation computes
[Munafo_thesis]_ (generalization of Eq. 3.48):

.. math::
   :label: eq_Keq_general

   \ln K_{\mathrm{eq}} = \sum_c \nu_c \left[
   \ln Q_c^{\mathrm{tra}} + \ln Q_c^{\mathrm{int}}
   + \ln m_c - \frac{e_{f,c}}{R_c \, T}
   \right]
   + \Delta\nu \ln\!\left(\frac{k_{\mathrm{B}} T}{p}\right)

where :math:`\Delta\nu = \sum_c \nu_c` accounts for the change in number
of moles.

**Alternative: Gibbs free energy path.** When NASA polynomials are enabled,
the equilibrium constant is computed from the Gibbs free energy
[Magin_thesis]_ (Eq. 2.3):

.. math::
   :label: th_eq_Keq_gibbs

   \ln K_{\mathrm{eq}} = -\frac{\Delta G^0(T)}{R_u T}
   = -\sum_c \nu_c \frac{g_c^0(T)}{R_u T}

Both paths (:eq:`eq_Keq_general` and :eq:`th_eq_Keq_gibbs`) yield the same
equilibrium constant to the extent that the underlying thermodynamic data
are consistent.

Source: ``src/thermo/thermo_equilibrium_comp.F90``, lines 504--541;
``src/thermo/thermo_prop_part_function.F90``.


.. _thermo_nasa:

NASA 9-Coefficient Polynomials
==============================

When the NASA polynomial path is enabled (``-DPLATO_ENABLE_NASA_POLY=ON``
or ``NASA_POLY_THERMO = T`` in the mixture file), thermodynamic properties
are computed from piecewise polynomial fits with 2--3 temperature intervals.
The reference state is :math:`T_{\mathrm{ref}} = 298.15` K,
:math:`p_{\mathrm{ref}} = 10^5` Pa (1 bar).

**Specific heat** (per mole):

.. math::
   :label: eq_nasa_cp

   \frac{c_p}{R_u} = \frac{a_1}{T^2} + \frac{a_2}{T} + a_3
   + a_4 T + a_5 T^2 + a_6 T^3 + a_7 T^4

**Enthalpy** (per mole):

.. math::
   :label: eq_nasa_h

   \frac{h}{R_u T} = -\frac{a_1}{T^2} + \frac{a_2 \ln T}{T} + a_3
   + \frac{a_4}{2} T + \frac{a_5}{3} T^2 + \frac{a_6}{4} T^3
   + \frac{a_7}{5} T^4 + \frac{b_1}{T}

**Entropy** (per mole, at :math:`p = p_{\mathrm{ref}}`):

.. math::
   :label: eq_nasa_s

   \frac{s}{R_u} = -\frac{a_1}{2T^2} - \frac{a_2}{T} + a_3 \ln T
   + a_4 T + \frac{a_5}{2} T^2 + \frac{a_6}{3} T^3
   + \frac{a_7}{4} T^4 + b_2

At arbitrary pressure:

.. math::

   s(T, p) = s(T, p_{\mathrm{ref}}) - R_u \ln\!\left(\frac{p}{p_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)

**Gibbs free energy** (per mole):

.. math::
   :label: eq_nasa_g

   \frac{g}{R_u T} = -\frac{a_1}{2T^2} - a_2 \frac{\ln T}{T}
   + a_3 (1 - \ln T)
   - \frac{a_4}{2} T - \frac{a_5}{6} T^2 - \frac{a_6}{12} T^3
   - \frac{a_7}{20} T^4 + \frac{b_1}{T} - b_2
   + \ln\!\left(\frac{p}{p_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)

.. note::

   Per-mass quantities are obtained by dividing by the molar mass :math:`M_i`.
   The thermodynamic identity :math:`g = h - T s` is satisfied to machine
   precision (~\ :math:`10^{-13}`) for both the NASA and partition-function
   paths; see :ref:`ex07_thermo`.

Source: ``src/thermo/nasa_thermo_prop_species_mole.F90``.


.. _thermo_multitemp:

Multi-Temperature Framework
============================

PLATO supports up to :math:`n_T` temperatures, each governing a different
energy mode. The physical justification is that **different energy modes
relax on different timescales**: translation equilibrates in a few collisions,
rotation in tens of collisions, vibration in thousands to millions, and
electronic excitation can be even slower.

.. list-table::
   :widths: 15 25 40
   :header-rows: 1

   * - Temperature
     - Modes
     - Species Types
   * - :math:`T_h`
     - Heavy-particle translation + rotation
     - All heavy species
   * - :math:`T_e`
     - Free-electron translation + electronic excitation
     - Electrons, atoms (electronic)
   * - :math:`T_v`
     - Vibration
     - Molecules
   * - :math:`T_r`
     - Rotation (if separate from :math:`T_h`)
     - Molecules

In the standard **two-temperature (2T)** model [Munafo_thesis]_ (Sec. 3.2):

- :math:`T_h` governs translation + rotation of all heavy particles.
- :math:`T_v = T_e` governs vibration + electronic excitation + electron
  translation.

In the **single-temperature (LTE)** limit: :math:`T_h = T_v = T_e = T`.

The ``TRANSFER`` database file specifies the number of temperatures and which
modes couple to which temperature.


Species Type Treatment
-----------------------

- **Free electrons** (atomicity = 0):
  :math:`c_v = \tfrac{3}{2} k_{\mathrm{B}}/m_e`,
  all properties evaluated at :math:`T_e`.
- **Atoms** (atomicity = 1): No rotational or vibrational modes.
  Translational at :math:`T_h`, electronic at :math:`T_e`.
- **Molecules** (atomicity > 1): All four mode types.
  Translational + rotational at :math:`T_h`, vibrational at :math:`T_v`,
  electronic at :math:`T_e`.


.. _thermo_formation:

Formation Enthalpies and Reference States
------------------------------------------

Species formation energies :math:`e_{f,i}` are defined at 0 K relative to
the elemental ground states. They enter:

1. The internal energy :eq:`eq_energy_total` as an additive constant
2. The partition function as a Boltzmann factor:
   :math:`Q_{\mathrm{form}} = \exp(-e_{f,i} m_i / (k_{\mathrm{B}} T))`
3. The equilibrium constant :eq:`eq_Keq_general` through the energy
   difference between products and reactants

For the N\ :sub:`2`--N system [Munafo_thesis]_ (Chapter 3), the dissociation
energy is :math:`D_0 = 2 E_{\mathrm{N}} - E_{\mathrm{N_2}(v=0,J=0)} = 9.75` eV,
where :math:`E_{\mathrm{N}}` is the atomic nitrogen formation energy.

The NASA polynomial path uses a reference state of :math:`T = 298.15` K,
:math:`p = 1` bar. PLATO converts between the two reference conventions
internally.
