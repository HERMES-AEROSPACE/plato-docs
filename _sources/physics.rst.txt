Physical Modeling and Theory
=============================

This chapter presents the theoretical framework underlying PLATO: the
governing equations for nonequilibrium multicomponent gas and plasma flows,
the thermodynamic and transport closures, the chemical kinetics models, and
the LTE simplification. Unless otherwise stated, the material follows
the reference paper [MunafoPanesi2025]_:

   Munafò & Panesi, "PLATO: a Library for the Thermodynamics, Transport, and
   Kinetics of Multicomponent Gases and Plasmas," *Journal of Thermophysics

   DOI: `10.2514/1.T7097 <https://doi.org/10.2514/1.T7097>`_


.. _phys_introduction:

Introduction
============

PLATO implements the **nonequilibrium kinetic theory for dilute gases**,
following the Chapman--Enskog (CE) method applied to the Boltzmann equation
[Chapman1970]_, [Ferziger1972]_. The key modeling assumptions are:

1. **Semiclassical framework** — classical translational energy, quantum
   internal energy (rotation, vibration, electronic excitation).
2. **Collision-dominated regime** — the gas is close enough to a local
   Maxwellian that the first-order CE expansion (Navier--Stokes level) is
   valid.
3. **Quasi-neutral plasma** — charge separation occurs only at the Debye
   scale; the plasma is electrically neutral at the continuum scale.
4. **Unmagnetized plasma** — the electron Hall parameter is small, so the
   magnetic field does not significantly modify transport coefficients.
5. **Maxwellian reaction regime** — transport properties are unaffected by
   inelastic collisions, except for resonant charge transfer which enters
   the collision integrals.

Within this framework, PLATO provides a hierarchy of physicochemical models
of increasing complexity:

- **Multitemperature (MT)** models — Park-style [Park1989]_, correlation-based.
- **Coarse-grained (CG)** reduced-order models — grouping of internal
  levels into bins.
- **Collisional-radiative (CR)** models — resolved electronic levels.
- **State-to-state (StS)** models — fully resolved rovibronic levels.

All models share the same governing equation structure; they differ in the
definition of what constitutes a "species" and in how internal energy modes
are described.


.. _phys_species_temp:

Species and Temperature Structure
=================================

Species Set
-----------

The species set is partitioned as

.. math::

   \mathcal{S} = \{e\} \cup \mathcal{H}

where :math:`e` denotes free electrons and :math:`\mathcal{H}` the heavy
particles (atoms, molecules, and their ions). The heavy-particle set is
further decomposed as :math:`\mathcal{H} = \mathcal{H}^n \cup
\mathcal{H}^c`, with :math:`\mathcal{H}^n` = neutrals and
:math:`\mathcal{H}^c` = charged heavy particles.

The meaning of "species" differs across model fidelity levels:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 20 80

   * - Model
     - Definition of species
   * - **MT**
     - Chemical components: N\ :sub:`2`, O, NO\ :sup:`+`, etc.
   * - **CG**
     - Grouped internal levels (bins): N\ :sub:`2`\ (g=1), N\ :sub:`2`\ (g=2), ...
   * - **CR**
     - Resolved electronic states: N(\ :sup:`4`\ S), N(\ :sup:`2`\ D), N(\ :sup:`2`\ P), ...
   * - **StS**
     - Fully resolved rovibronic levels: N\ :sub:`2`\ (v, J, el), ...

This distinction is central to PLATO's design: the same transport and
kinetics algorithms operate on any species set, from 5-species air
(MT) to thousands of pseudo-species (StS).

Temperature Structure
---------------------

The gas is characterized by the following temperatures:

- :math:`T_h` — heavy-particle translational temperature.
- :math:`T_e` — free-electron translational temperature (distinct from
  :math:`T_h` due to the large electron-heavy mass disparity).
- :math:`\tilde{T}_1, \ldots, \tilde{T}_m` — nonequilibrium internal-mode
  temperatures (vibrational, rotational, electronic), used in MT and CG
  models only.

The set :math:`\mathcal{I}` denotes the internal degrees of freedom that
are in thermal nonequilibrium with translation. In CR/StS models,
:math:`\mathcal{I} = \emptyset` because the populations of individual
levels are tracked explicitly.

**Two-temperature model (Park)**:
The most common MT specialization sets :math:`T_e = \tilde{T}_v =
\tilde{T}_{el} \equiv T_{ve}` (vibronic temperature), so that a single
additional equation (for the combined vibro-electronic energy) replaces the
separate internal-mode and electron-energy equations.


.. _phys_governing_eqs:

Governing Equations
===================

NLTE System
-----------

The full nonequilibrium system comprises the following conservation
equations.

**Species continuity** (:math:`s \in \mathcal{S}`):

.. math::
   :label: eq_species

   \frac{\partial \rho_s}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot \bigl[\rho_s (\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{U}_s)\bigr]
   = \omega_s

where :math:`\rho_s` is the species partial density, :math:`\mathbf{v}` is
the mass-averaged velocity, :math:`\mathbf{U}_s` is the species diffusion
velocity, and :math:`\omega_s` is the mass production rate.

**Global momentum**:

.. math::
   :label: eq_momentum

   \frac{\partial (\rho \mathbf{v})}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{v} \otimes \mathbf{v} + p\,\mathbf{I})
   = \nabla \cdot \boldsymbol{\tau}
   + \mathbf{J} \times \mathbf{B}

where :math:`p` is the total pressure, :math:`\boldsymbol{\tau}` is the
viscous stress tensor, :math:`\mathbf{J}` is the current density, and
:math:`\mathbf{B}` is the magnetic field.

**Global energy**:

.. math::
   :label: eq_energy

   \frac{\partial (\rho E)}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot (\rho H \mathbf{v})
   = \nabla \cdot (\boldsymbol{\tau} \cdot \mathbf{v} - \mathbf{q})
   + \Omega + \mathbf{J} \cdot \mathbf{E}

where :math:`E = e + \tfrac{1}{2}|\mathbf{v}|^2` is the total energy per
unit mass, :math:`H = h + \tfrac{1}{2}|\mathbf{v}|^2` is the total
enthalpy, :math:`\mathbf{q}` is the total heat flux, and :math:`\Omega`
is the volumetric energy source (e.g., radiation).

**Nonequilibrium internal energy** (for each mode
:math:`i \in \mathcal{I}`):

.. math::
   :label: eq_internal

   \frac{\partial (\rho e_i)}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot (\rho e_i \mathbf{v})
   = -\nabla \cdot \mathbf{q}_i + \Omega_i

where :math:`e_i` is the internal energy per unit mass for mode :math:`i`,
:math:`\mathbf{q}_i` is the internal-mode heat flux, and :math:`\Omega_i`
is the energy exchange source term for that mode.

**Free-electron energy**:

.. math::
   :label: eq_electron

   \frac{\partial (\rho_e \varepsilon_e)}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot (\rho_e \varepsilon_e \mathbf{v})
   = -\nabla \cdot \mathbf{q}_e - p_e\,\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}
   + \Omega_e + \mathbf{J}_e \cdot \mathbf{E}'

where :math:`\varepsilon_e = \tfrac{3}{2}(k_B/m_e)T_e` is the electron
translational energy per unit mass, :math:`p_e = n_e k_B T_e` is the
electron pressure, :math:`\Omega_e` accounts for energy exchange with heavy
particles, and :math:`\mathbf{E}' = \mathbf{E} + \mathbf{v} \times
\mathbf{B}` is the electric field in the hydrodynamic frame.

LTE System
----------

Under local thermodynamic equilibrium (LTE), all temperatures are equal
(:math:`T_h = T_e = \tilde{T}_i \equiv T`), the composition is determined
by the law of mass action, and the species and internal energy equations are
replaced by algebraic relations. The governing equations reduce to:

**Global mass**:

.. math::
   :label: eq_lte_mass

   \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{v}) = 0

**Global momentum**:

.. math::
   :label: eq_lte_momentum

   \frac{\partial (\rho \mathbf{v})}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{v} \otimes \mathbf{v} + p\,\mathbf{I})
   = \nabla \cdot \boldsymbol{\tau}

**Global energy**:

.. math::
   :label: eq_lte_energy

   \frac{\partial (\rho E)}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot (\rho H \mathbf{v})
   = \nabla \cdot (\boldsymbol{\tau} \cdot \mathbf{v} - \mathbf{q})


.. _phys_thermodynamics:

Thermodynamics
==============

Equation of State
-----------------

The mixture is an ideal gas obeying Dalton's law:

.. math::
   :label: eq_dalton

   p = p_h + p_e = n_h k_B T_h + n_e k_B T_e

where :math:`n_h = \sum_{s \in \mathcal{H}} n_s` is the total heavy-particle
number density and :math:`n_e` is the free-electron number density. For a
single-temperature gas (:math:`T_h = T_e = T`), this simplifies to
:math:`p = n k_B T`.

The total number density in terms of pressure and temperatures:

.. math::
   :label: eq_number_density

   n = \frac{p}{k_B T_h \bigl[1 + x_e (T_e / T_h - 1)\bigr]}

where :math:`x_e = n_e / n` is the electron mole fraction.

Energy Content
--------------

The total internal energy per unit mass is decomposed as:

.. math::
   :label: eq_energy_decomp

   e = \sum_{s \in \mathcal{H}} y_s
       \bigl[e^{tr}_s(T_h) + e^*_s(T_h, \tilde{T}_1, \ldots)\bigr]
     + \sum_{s \in \mathcal{S}} y_s\, e^f_s
     + \sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}} e_i
     + \varepsilon_e

where :math:`y_s = \rho_s / \rho` is the mass fraction, :math:`e^{tr}_s` is
the translational energy, :math:`e^*_s` collects the internal mode energies,
:math:`e^f_s` is the formation energy at the reference temperature, and
:math:`\varepsilon_e` is the free-electron translational energy.

**Total energy and enthalpy**:

.. math::

   E = e + \tfrac{1}{2}|\mathbf{v}|^2, \qquad
   H = h + \tfrac{1}{2}|\mathbf{v}|^2, \qquad
   h = e + p/\rho

Internal Energy Models
----------------------

PLATO provides two thermodynamic paths, selected at configure time.

**Path 1: Partition-function-based (default)**

The species internal energy is computed from statistical mechanics using
the partition function :math:`Z_s(T)`:

.. math::

   e^{int}_s(T) = \frac{k_B T^2}{m_s}
   \frac{\partial \ln Z_s}{\partial T}

The partition function is built from the following contributions:

*Translational energy* (3 degrees of freedom):

.. math::
   :label: eq_e_trans

   e^{tr}_s(T) = \frac{3}{2}\frac{k_B}{m_s}\,T

*Rigid-rotor rotational energy* (linear molecules):

.. math::
   :label: eq_e_rot

   e^{rot}_s(T_r) = \frac{k_B}{m_s}\,T_r

*Harmonic-oscillator vibrational energy*:

.. math::
   :label: eq_e_vib

   e^{vib}_s(T_v) = \frac{k_B}{m_s}
   \frac{\theta^v_s}{\exp(\theta^v_s / T_v) - 1}

where :math:`\theta^v_s` is the characteristic vibrational temperature.

*Electronic energy* (Boltzmann-weighted sum over levels):

.. math::
   :label: eq_e_elec

   e^{el}_s(T_{el}) = \frac{1}{m_s}
   \frac{\sum_k g_k\, \epsilon_k\, \exp(-\epsilon_k / k_B T_{el})}
        {\sum_k g_k\, \exp(-\epsilon_k / k_B T_{el})}

where :math:`g_k` and :math:`\epsilon_k` are the degeneracy and energy of
electronic level :math:`k`.

*Formation energy*:

.. math::

   e^f_s = \Delta h^0_{f,s}(T_{ref}) - \frac{k_B T_{ref}}{m_s}

where :math:`\Delta h^0_{f,s}` is the standard enthalpy of formation at the
reference temperature :math:`T_{ref} = 298.15` K.

The ``MODEL`` parameter in the thermodynamic data files selects which
contributions are included:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 20 50 30

   * - MODEL
     - Components
     - Typical species
   * - ``RR_HO_EL``
     - Rigid rotor + harmonic oscillator + electronic
     - Molecules (MT)
   * - ``EL``
     - Electronic Boltzmann only
     - Atoms (MT)
   * - ``RR``, ``HO``, ``RR_HO``
     - Partial combinations
     - Molecules
   * - ``NONE`` / ``STS``
     - :math:`Z = g`, :math:`e = 0` (level as pseudo-species)
     - CR/StS levels
   * - ``GROUP`` / ``RR_GROUP``
     - Table interpolation (coarse-grained bins)
     - CG bins

**Path 2: NASA 9-coefficient polynomials** (``--enable-nasa_poly`` /
``-DPLATO_ENABLE_NASA_POLY=ON``)

Thermodynamic properties are computed from piecewise polynomial fits:

.. math::

   \frac{C_p(T)}{R_s} = a_1 T^{-2} + a_2 T^{-1} + a_3
   + a_4\,T + a_5\,T^2 + a_6\,T^3 + a_7\,T^4

with analogous expressions for enthalpy :math:`h(T)` and entropy
:math:`s(T)` (including integration constants :math:`a_8`, :math:`a_9`).
This path provides entropy and Gibbs free energy, which are not available
from the partition-function path.

LTE Secondary Properties
-------------------------

At chemical equilibrium, secondary thermodynamic properties are obtained
from the equation of state and composition derivatives. Because the
composition itself depends on :math:`T` and :math:`p`, these derivatives
involve the response of the equilibrium solver.

*Specific heats*:

.. math::

   C_p = T\left(\frac{\partial s}{\partial T}\right)_p, \qquad
   C_v = C_p - \frac{\alpha^2 T}{\rho\,\beta_T}

*Coefficient of thermal expansion and isothermal compressibility*:

.. math::

   \alpha = -\frac{1}{\rho}\left(\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial T}\right)_p
          = \frac{1}{T} + \frac{1}{R}\left(\frac{\partial R}{\partial T}\right)_p

.. math::

   \beta_T = \frac{1}{\rho}\left(\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial p}\right)_T
           = \frac{1}{p} - \frac{1}{R}\left(\frac{\partial R}{\partial p}\right)_T

where :math:`R = k_B / \bar{m}` is the mixture-specific gas constant.

*Equilibrium speed of sound*:

.. math::
   :label: eq_speed_of_sound

   c_{eq} = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma_{eq}}{\rho\,\beta_T}}, \qquad
   \gamma_{eq} = \frac{C_p}{C_v}

*Generalized adiabatic exponents* [Mihalas1984]_:

.. math::
   :label: eq_gamma1

   \Gamma_1 = \left(\frac{\partial \ln p}{\partial \ln \rho}\right)_s
            = c_{eq}^2 \frac{\rho}{p}

.. math::
   :label: eq_gamma2

   \frac{\Gamma_2}{\Gamma_2 - 1}
   = \left(\frac{\partial \ln p}{\partial \ln T}\right)_s
   = \frac{C_p\,\rho}{\alpha\,p}

.. math::
   :label: eq_gamma3

   \Gamma_3 - 1
   = \left(\frac{\partial \ln T}{\partial \ln \rho}\right)_s
   = \frac{\alpha}{\rho\,C_v\,\beta_T}

These reduce to :math:`\Gamma_1 = \Gamma_2 = \Gamma_3 = \gamma` for a
calorically perfect gas.

Equilibrium Composition
-----------------------

The LTE composition is computed by solving a nonlinear system combining:

1. Element conservation (atom-balance constraints)
2. Normalization (:math:`\sum X_s = 1`)
3. Law of mass action (equilibrium constants :math:`K_{eq}` from partition
   function ratios or NASA Gibbs free energy)

PLATO uses a Newton--Raphson solver in log-space following
[Bottin1999]_, with Schur-complement reduction. Convergence criterion:
:math:`\log_{10}(\|r\|_2) < -12`. A Debye--Huckel corrected variant is
available for ionized plasmas (ionization potential lowering, Coulomb
pressure correction, partition function truncation via Griem's criterion).


.. _phys_transport:

Transport Properties
====================

All transport properties are derived from the first-order Chapman--Enskog
solution to the Boltzmann equation [Magin2004]_. The resulting expressions
involve collision integrals (see :ref:`phys_collision_integrals`) and are
solved as linear systems of the form
:math:`\mathbf{G}\,\mathbf{z} = \mathbf{b}`, where :math:`\mathbf{G}`
depends on species mole fractions and collision integrals.

Viscosity
---------

Newton's law for the viscous stress tensor:

.. math::
   :label: eq_viscosity

   \boldsymbol{\tau} = \mu\bigl(\nabla\mathbf{v} + \nabla\mathbf{v}^T
   - \tfrac{2}{3}\,\mathbf{I}\,\nabla\cdot\mathbf{v}\bigr)

The shear viscosity :math:`\mu` is computed from the heavy-particle
subsystem at 1st Sonine order:

.. math::

   \mu = \mathbf{z}^\mu \cdot \mathbf{x}_h, \qquad
   \mathbf{G}^\mu\,\mathbf{z}^\mu = \mathbf{x}_h

where :math:`\mathbf{x}_h` is the vector of heavy-particle mole fractions
and :math:`\mathbf{G}^\mu` is a symmetric positive-definite
:math:`n_h \times n_h` matrix depending on :math:`\bar{Q}^{(l,r)}_{sp}`
collision integrals. The system is solved by Cholesky (LDL\ :sup:`T`)
decomposition.

Heat Flux
---------

The total heat flux has three contributions:

.. math::
   :label: eq_heat_flux

   \mathbf{q} = \underbrace{-(\lambda^{tr}_h + \lambda^*_h)\nabla T_h
   + \sum_{s \in \mathcal{H}} \rho_s\,\mathbf{U}_s\,h_s}_{\text{heavy}}
   + \underbrace{\sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}} \mathbf{q}_i}_{\text{internal modes}}
   + \underbrace{\mathbf{q}_e}_{\text{electron}}

*Internal-mode heat flux* (for :math:`i \in \mathcal{I}`):

.. math::

   \mathbf{q}_i = -\lambda^i_h\,\nabla\tilde{T}_i
   + \sum_{s \in \mathcal{H}} \rho_s\,\mathbf{U}_s\,e^i_s

*Free-electron heat flux*:

.. math::

   \mathbf{q}_e = -\lambda^{tr}_e\,\nabla T_e
   + \rho_e\,\mathbf{U}_e\,h^{tr}_e
   + n k_B T_h \sum_{s \in \mathcal{S}} \kappa^e_{T,s}\,\mathbf{U}_s

where :math:`\kappa^e_{T,s}` are the electron thermal diffusion ratios.

Thermal Conductivity
--------------------

**Heavy translational** :math:`\lambda^{tr}_h` (1st Sonine):

.. math::

   \lambda^{tr}_h = \mathbf{z}^\lambda \cdot \mathbf{x}_h, \qquad
   \mathbf{G}^\lambda\,\mathbf{z}^\lambda = \mathbf{x}_h

Symmetric :math:`n_h \times n_h` system, solved by Cholesky.

**Internal modes** :math:`\lambda^*_h` (generalized Eucken correction):

.. math::

   \lambda^*_h = \sum_{s \in \mathcal{H}} \frac{x_s\,C^{int}_{v,s}\,\mu_s}
   {m_s}

where :math:`C^{int}_{v,s}` is the internal specific heat at constant volume
and :math:`\mu_s` is the pure-species viscosity.

**Electron translational** :math:`\lambda^{tr}_e` (3rd Sonine —
higher order needed for accuracy):

.. math::
   :label: eq_lambda_e

   \lambda^{tr}_e = \frac{75}{64}\,k_B\,
   \frac{x_e^2 \sqrt{2\pi k_B T_e / m_e}}
   {\Lambda^{11}_{ee} - (\Lambda^{12}_{ee})^2 / \Lambda^{22}_{ee}}

where :math:`\Lambda^{ij}_{ee}` are 2\ :sup:`nd` and 3\ :sup:`rd` Sonine
expansion coefficients involving electron-heavy collision integrals. The
expression is evaluated analytically (2x2 matrix inversion).

**Reactive thermal conductivity** (LTE only):

.. math::
   :label: eq_lambda_rea

   \lambda^{rea} = -\sum_{s \in \mathcal{S}} \rho_s\,\tilde{U}_s\,
   h_s(T;\, T_{ref})

where :math:`\tilde{U}_s` are the equilibrium diffusion velocities driven
by :math:`\nabla T` alone (computed by solving the Stefan--Maxwell system
with the chemical potential gradient as driving force). This can be the
dominant contribution to thermal conductivity in dissociating/ionizing
regimes.

Diffusion Velocities (Stefan--Maxwell)
--------------------------------------

The species diffusion velocities :math:`\mathbf{U}_s` are obtained from
the Stefan--Maxwell equations. For heavy particles
:math:`s \in \mathcal{H}`:

.. math::
   :label: eq_stefan_maxwell_heavy

   \sum_{p \in \mathcal{S}} G^U_{sp}\,\mathbf{U}_p
   - \kappa_s\,\mathbf{E}'
   = -\mathbf{d}'_s

For electrons:

.. math::
   :label: eq_stefan_maxwell_electron

   \sum_{p \in \mathcal{S}} G^U_{ep}\,\mathbf{U}_p
   - \frac{T_h}{T_e}\,\kappa_e\,\mathbf{E}'
   = -\frac{T_h}{T_e}\,\mathbf{d}'_e

The modified driving forces include pressure, concentration, and thermal
diffusion:

.. math::

   \mathbf{d}'_s = \frac{1}{n k_B T_h}
   \bigl[\nabla(n_s k_B T_s) - y_s\,p\,\nabla\ln p\bigr]
   + \frac{\kappa^h_{T,s}}{T_h}\,\nabla T_h
   + \frac{\kappa^e_{T,s}\,T_s}{T_e^2}\,\nabla T_e

Two constraints close the system: (i) mass conservation
:math:`\sum_{s \in \mathcal{S}} \rho_s\,\mathbf{U}_s = 0`, and
(ii) **ambipolar diffusion** (no net current in the absence of an
external field):

.. math::

   \sum_{s \in \mathcal{S}} \kappa_s\,\mathbf{U}_s = 0

For **neutral mixtures**, the Stefan--Maxwell matrix is symmetric and solved
by Cholesky decomposition (:math:`n_c \times n_c`). For **ionized
mixtures**, the matrix is non-symmetric (:math:`(n_c+1) \times (n_c+1)`,
including the ambipolar constraint) and solved by LU factorization (Crout's
method).

Thermal Diffusion Ratios
------------------------

The thermal diffusion ratios :math:`\kappa^h_{T,s}` (heavy) and
:math:`\kappa^e_{T,s}` (electron) appear in the Stefan--Maxwell driving
forces and in the heat flux. They are computed from the same collision
integral data using auxiliary linear systems at 1st Sonine order.

Electrical Conductivity
-----------------------

**Full expression** (Ohm's law):

.. math::

   \sigma = \sum_{s,p \in \mathcal{S}} n_s\,q_s\,D_{sp}\,\kappa_p

where :math:`D_{sp}` are multicomponent diffusion coefficients and
:math:`\kappa_s = n_s q_s / (n k_B T_s)` are the reduced charges.

**Electron approximation** (usually sufficient):

.. math::
   :label: eq_sigma_e

   \sigma_e = \frac{3}{16}\,\frac{x_e^2\,q_e^2}{k_B T_e}\,
   \sqrt{\frac{2\pi k_B T_e}{m_e}}\,
   \frac{\Lambda^{11}_{ee}}
   {\Lambda^{00}_{ee}\,\Lambda^{11}_{ee} - (\Lambda^{01}_{ee})^2}

PLATO computes :math:`\sigma_e` at 2nd Sonine order by default (analytic
2x2 inversion), with the option of 3rd-order (Cholesky 3x3).

**Generalized Ohm's law**:

.. math::

   \mathbf{J} \approx \mathbf{J}_e
   \approx \sigma_e\!\left(\mathbf{E}'
   - \frac{\nabla p_e}{n_e\,q_e}\right)

.. _phys_collision_integrals:

Collision Integrals
===================

All transport properties are ultimately built from collision integrals,
which encode the microscopic physics of binary interactions.

Definition
----------

The reduced collision integrals are defined as:

.. math::
   :label: eq_colint

   \bar{Q}^{(l,r)}_{sp}
   = \frac{4(l+1)}{(r+1)!\,[2l + 1 - (-1)^l]}
   \int_0^\infty e^{-\gamma^2}\,\gamma^{2r+3}\,Q^l_{sp}(\gamma)\,d\gamma

where :math:`\gamma^2 = \tfrac{1}{2} m_{sp} g^2 / (k_B T_{sp})` is the
reduced collision energy, :math:`g` is the relative velocity,
:math:`m_{sp} = m_s m_p / (m_s + m_p)` is the reduced mass,
:math:`T_{sp}` is the effective temperature for the :math:`(s,p)` pair,
and :math:`Q^l_{sp}` is the :math:`l`-th order transport cross-section
[Ferziger1972]_.

.. note::

   The notation :math:`\bar{Q}^{(l,r)}_{sp} = \pi\sigma^2_{sp}\,
   \Omega^{(l,r)*}_{sp}` absorbs the reference cross-section
   :math:`\pi\sigma^2_{sp}` into the definition, following the convention
   used in PLATO and EGLIB. This differs from some references where
   :math:`\Omega^{(l,r)*}_{sp}` is dimensionless.

Derived Ratios
--------------

The following ratios of collision integrals appear in the transport
coefficient matrices:

.. math::

   A^*_{sp} = \frac{\bar{Q}^{(2,2)}_{sp}}{\bar{Q}^{(1,1)}_{sp}}, \qquad
   B^*_{sp} = \frac{5\bar{Q}^{(1,2)}_{sp} - 4\bar{Q}^{(1,3)}_{sp}}
              {\bar{Q}^{(1,1)}_{sp}}, \qquad
   C^*_{sp} = \frac{\bar{Q}^{(1,2)}_{sp}}{\bar{Q}^{(1,1)}_{sp}}

Binary Diffusion Coefficients
-----------------------------

The binary diffusion coefficient for the :math:`(s,p)` pair:

.. math::
   :label: eq_bin_diff

   D_{sp} = \frac{3}{16\,n\,\bar{Q}^{(1,1)}_{sp}}
   \sqrt{\frac{2\pi k_B T_{sp}(m_s + m_p)}{m_s m_p}}

Interaction Models
------------------

PLATO stores precomputed collision integrals on a uniform temperature grid
(default 200--100200 K, loaded at initialization from ``database/transport/``).
Multiple interaction models are available:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 20 25 30 25

   * - Interaction
     - Default model
     - Alternative
     - Source files
   * - Neutral--neutral
     - Gupta et al. curve fits [Gupta1991]_
     - Lennard-Jones
     - ``HH_NN``
   * - Neutral--ion
     - Gupta et al. curve fits
     - Polarization/Langevin + resonant charge exchange
     - ``HH_NI``
   * - Electron--neutral
     - Gupta et al. curve fits
     - Capitelli (Laricchiuta/Bruno) [Bruno2002]_, Andre 7-parameter
     - ``eN``
   * - Charged--charged
     - Screened Coulomb
     - —
     - Computed at runtime (Debye length)

At runtime, neutral-heavy and electron-heavy integrals use **linear
interpolation** on the precomputed tables. Charged-charged integrals are
computed **analytically** at each call via polynomial fits to
Mason--Munn--Smith data [Mason1967]_, since they depend on the Debye
screening length (which varies with the local plasma state).


.. _phys_kinetics:

Chemical Kinetics
=================

PLATO computes mass production rates :math:`\omega_s` and energy exchange
terms :math:`\Omega_i` from a database of elementary reactions.

Collisional Processes
---------------------

The reference paper classifies eight types of collisional processes:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 5 20 45 15 15

   * - #
     - Type
     - Example
     - Temperature
     - Model
   * - 1
     - Dissociation (heavy impact)
     - N\ :sub:`2` + M :math:`\rightleftharpoons` N + N + M
     - :math:`T_h` or :math:`T_{Tv}`
     - MT / StS
   * - 2
     - Dissociation (electron impact)
     - N\ :sub:`2` + e :math:`\rightleftharpoons` N + N + e
     - :math:`T_e`
     - MT / StS
   * - 3
     - Predissociation
     - N\ :sub:`2`\ (A) :math:`\rightarrow` N + N
     - :math:`T_h`
     - StS
   * - 4
     - Excitation (heavy impact)
     - N(i) + M :math:`\rightleftharpoons` N(j) + M
     - :math:`T_h`
     - CR / StS
   * - 5
     - Excitation (electron impact)
     - N(i) + e :math:`\rightleftharpoons` N(j) + e
     - :math:`T_e`
     - CR / StS
   * - 6
     - Ionization (electron impact)
     - N + e :math:`\rightleftharpoons` N\ :sup:`+` + e + e
     - :math:`T_e`
     - MT / StS
   * - 7
     - Associative ionization
     - N + O :math:`\rightleftharpoons` NO\ :sup:`+` + e
     - :math:`T_h`
     - MT
   * - 8
     - Particle/charge exchange
     - N\ :sub:`2` + O :math:`\rightleftharpoons` NO + N
     - :math:`T_h`
     - MT (Zel'dovich)

Additionally, PLATO supports radiative processes:

- **Bound-bound** line emission/absorption (line-by-line with Voigt profiles)
- **Bound-free** photoionization (Kramers hydrogenic cross-section)
- **Free-free** Bremsstrahlung (electron-ion Coulomb + electron-neutral)
- **Radiative recombination**: N\ :sup:`+` + e :math:`\rightarrow`
  N + :math:`h\nu`

Rate Coefficients
-----------------

**Forward rates** use the modified Arrhenius form:

.. math::
   :label: eq_arrhenius

   k_f(T) = A\,T^n\,\exp\!\left(-\frac{E_a}{k_B T}\right)

where :math:`A`, :math:`n`, :math:`E_a` are read from the kinetics
database file. The activation temperature :math:`c = E_a / k_B` is
stored directly.

**MT dissociation**: For reactions governed by the Park two-temperature
model, the controlling temperature is the geometric mean:

.. math::

   T_{Tv} = T_h^{1 - \alpha}\,T_{ve}^{\alpha}

with :math:`\alpha` typically between 0.3 and 0.7.

Microreversibility and Detailed Balance
----------------------------------------

**Backward rates are never read from the database.** They are always
computed from detailed balance:

.. math::
   :label: eq_microreversibility

   k_b(T) = \frac{k_f(T)}{K_{eq}(T)}

The equilibrium constant :math:`K_{eq}` is obtained from:

- **Partition function ratios** (default path):

  .. math::

     K_{eq}(T) = \prod_s \left(\frac{Z_s(T)}{V}\right)^{\nu_s}
     \exp\!\left(-\frac{\Delta e^f}{k_B T}\right)

  where :math:`\nu_s` are stoichiometric coefficients and
  :math:`\Delta e^f` is the formation energy difference.

- **NASA Gibbs free energy** (``--enable-nasa_poly``):

  .. math::

     \ln K_{eq}(T) = -\frac{\Delta G^0(T)}{R\,T}

For **CR/StS** models, the level-specific reverse rate follows directly:

.. math::

   k_{ji}(T_e) = k_{ij}(T_e)\,\frac{g_i}{g_j}\,
   \exp\!\left(\frac{e^f_j - e^f_i}{k_B T_e}\right)

where :math:`g_i`, :math:`g_j` are level degeneracies and :math:`e^f_i`,
:math:`e^f_j` are level formation energies per particle.

Mass Production Terms
---------------------

The source term pipeline in PLATO:

1. Convert partial densities :math:`\rho_s \to n_s`.
2. Clamp temperatures (enforce positivity).
3. Compute forward rate coefficients :math:`k_f` and backward rates
   :math:`k_b = k_f / K_{eq}`.
4. Loop over reaction categories, accumulate:

   .. math::

      \dot{n}_s = \sum_r \nu_{s,r}\bigl(k_{f,r}\,\Pi_{\text{react}}
                  - k_{b,r}\,\Pi_{\text{prod}}\bigr)

5. Convert to mass production rates: :math:`\omega_s = m_s\,\dot{n}_s`.

Energy Exchange Terms
---------------------

PLATO computes six energy exchange mechanisms:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 10 25 65

   * - Term
     - Physics
     - Model
   * - :math:`\Omega^{VT}`
     - Vibration--Translation
     - Landau--Teller with Millikan--White relaxation times + Park
       high-temperature correction
   * - :math:`\Omega^{VE}`
     - Vibration--Electron
     - Piecewise polynomial :math:`\tau_{VE}(p_e, T_e)` for
       N\ :sub:`2`--e
   * - :math:`\Omega^{TE}`
     - Elastic electron--heavy
     - :math:`\Omega^{TE} = \tfrac{3}{2} n_e k_B (T_h - T_e) / \tau_{eh}`
       (see below)
   * - :math:`\Omega^{CV}`
     - Chemistry--Vibration coupling
     - :math:`\Omega^{CV} = \sum_m \omega_m\,e^v_m(T_v)` (post-kinetics)
   * - :math:`\Omega^{CEL}`
     - Chemistry--Electronic coupling
     - :math:`\Omega^{CEL} = \sum_s \omega_s\,e^{el}_s(T_e)` (post-kinetics)
   * - :math:`\Omega^{ff}`
     - Free-free Bremsstrahlung
     - Kramers formula (Gaunt factor = 1)

**Electron-heavy elastic energy exchange** :math:`\Omega^{TE}`:

Following [Petschek1957]_, the energy transferred from heavy particles to
free electrons by elastic collisions is:

.. math::
   :label: eq_omega_te

   \Omega^{TE} = \frac{3}{2}\,n_e\,k_B\,\frac{T_h - T_e}{\tau_{eh}}

where the electron--heavy relaxation time is:

.. math::
   :label: eq_tau_eh

   \frac{1}{\tau_{eh}} = \frac{8}{3}
   \sqrt{\frac{8 k_B T_e}{\pi m_e}}
   \sum_{s \in \mathcal{H}} \frac{m_e}{m_s}\,n_s\,
   \bar{Q}^{(1,1)}_{es}

This expression is derived from the Maxwellian-averaged momentum-transfer
cross-section; the full derivation is given in PLATO's internal theory
document (``doc/theory/doc.tex``).

Analytical Jacobians
--------------------

PLATO provides the full analytical Jacobian:

.. math::

   \mathbf{J} = \frac{\partial(\boldsymbol{\omega},\,\boldsymbol{\Omega})}
   {\partial(\boldsymbol{\rho},\,\mathbf{T})}

including chain-rule contributions for the Park activation temperature
:math:`T_{Tv}` and all energy exchange mechanisms. The mass-only Jacobian
(:math:`n_s \times n_s`) is also available. For coarse-grained (CG) models,
a **numerical** Jacobian (finite differences) is used due to the tabulated
rate coefficients.


.. _phys_lte_formulation:

LTE Formulation
===============

Under LTE, the governing equations reduce to the Euler/Navier--Stokes
system (Eqs. :eq:`eq_lte_mass`--:eq:`eq_lte_energy`) with the following
closures.

Simplified Transport
--------------------

The viscous stress tensor retains Newton's law (Eq. :eq:`eq_viscosity`),
but the heat flux simplifies to:

.. math::

   \mathbf{q} = -\lambda\,\nabla T

with total thermal conductivity:

.. math::

   \lambda = \lambda^{tr} + \lambda^{int} + \lambda^{rea}

The **reactive thermal conductivity** :math:`\lambda^{rea}` (Eq.
:eq:`eq_lambda_rea`) is computed by solving the Stefan--Maxwell system with
the chemical potential gradient as the sole driving force and projecting
the resulting diffusion velocities onto :math:`\nabla T`. This term can
dominate the total conductivity in temperature ranges where dissociation or
ionization is active.

Equilibrium Composition Solver
------------------------------

The composition at each (:math:`p`, :math:`T`) or (:math:`\rho`, :math:`T`)
point is obtained from:

1. **Element conservation**: :math:`\sum_s \nu_{es}\,X_s = X_e^0` for each
   element :math:`e`.
2. **Normalization**: :math:`\sum_s X_s = 1`.
3. **Law of mass action**: :math:`K_{eq,r}(T) = \prod_s X_s^{\nu_{sr}}
   \cdot (p / p_0)^{\Delta\nu_r}`.

The system is solved by Newton--Raphson iteration in the logarithms of the
unknowns (ensuring positivity). A Schur complement reduction exploits the
block structure of the Jacobian (element and species blocks). Convergence
is typically achieved in 3--5 iterations for well-conditioned problems.
